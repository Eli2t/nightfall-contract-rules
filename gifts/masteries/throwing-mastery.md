# Throwing Mastery

*Throw things with ease*

**Category:** Masteries

**Vectors:** Passive

**Modalities:** Power

## Description

This Power grants access to an array of powers relating to the usage of thrown weapons (as well as occasionally objects thrown outside of combat).

## Enhancements

- **Assassin**
  If your attack Injures your target, they cannot make a sound, even incidentally, for a single Round.
  *Errata: Even the sound of their body and equipment dropping to the ground is silenced.*

- **Boomerang**
  On your Initiative, in the Round following a thrown attack, the objects you threw will return back to you. You may catch them automatically, without making a roll or spending an Action.

- **Bullet Parry**
  You may use a Reaction to Defend against any Melee, thrown, firearm, or projectile Attack in range of your Attacks.

- **Called it**
  Called Shots that you attempt only require a Contested Outcome of 2 for small targets and 4 for tiny targets.

- **Catcher In The Rye**
  You may catch anything flying within arm’s reach without a roll and without using a Reaction, as long as it is not an Attack. You may juggle a number of throwable objects equal to your Dexterity as a Free Action.

- **Finesse**
  When Attacking a single being, you may limit the Severity of any Injury you inflict or determine a maximum Wound Level, including Incapacitated. The Injury is treated as normal for Battle Scars and Stabilization

- **Flurry**
  You may draw any throwing weapon that is on your person or unattended within arm’s reach as a Free Action. You may make a full-auto sweep Attack at +2 Difficulty, covering 5 square feet per 3 projectiles thrown.
  *Errata: Use the firearms rules for full-auto sweeps. Applies only to projectiles that weigh under 25 pounds.*

- **It's All About Reflexes** [Seasoned]
  You may use your throwing roll to Defend or Clash against any projectile Attack, even when unarmed. If you succeed a Defense, you catch their projectile. If you succeed a Clash, you make a throwing Attack against the target using the projectile.

- **Jackie Chan**
  You do not receive a Difficulty penalty when throwing objects that are not designed for throwing.

- **Junk Wolf**
  Thrown attacks with improvised thrown weapons always deal a minimum of 3 Weapon Damage.
  Requires: enhancements: jackie-chan

- **Launch** [Veteran]
  As long as you are outside, you may Exert your Mind and spend an Action to throw any Object you can lift without Concentrating into orbit.
  Requires: enhancements: nobody-tosses-dwarf

- **Nobody Tosses A Dwarf**
  Anything you can lift, even if doing so requires Concentration, you can throw 50 feet. Weapon damage for a heavy improvised thrown weapon is equal to the Brawn required to lift it minus 2 (minimum 0).

- **Pin Down**
  If one of your Attacks with something sharp injures a target near a wall or floor, they are pinned to that surface. Safely removing the weapon costs a Quick Action. If they move before removing it, the Injury’s Severity worsens by 1.

- **Ricochet**
  You can make a thrown attack against a target behind cover so long as there is at least one exposed pathway to them.

- **Straight To The Face**
  You may make a throwing attack at melee range with no penalty.

- **Track And Field**
  If you move at least 10 feet in a straight line before making a thrown Attack in that direction, its effective range is increased by double the distance you moved, and it deals +1 Damage.

- **Yeet!**
  You can throw things up to 100 extra feet per point of Brawn with no penalty. Anything thrown further than 200 feet will not land at its target until the next Round on your initiative.
  *Errata: When throwing further than 200 feet, you may throw where you expect a stationary or predictably-moving target to be, but if they move in an unexpected way, the Attack will miss.*

## Drawbacks

- **Conditional** [Passive | Stackable]
  This Effect is only available when a certain condition is met. This condition must be severe enough that this Gift is unusable the majority of the time.
  *Errata: The condition must be significant. Subject to GM approval.*
  Detail required: Condition

- **Critical Failure** [Passive]
  Roll a single D10 when you activate this Effect.  If you roll a 1, you critically fail in a way that is extraordinarily dangerous. Failure should cause at least a Severity-4 Injury, a Major Battle Scar, or an Effect counter to its purpose.
  *Errata: Other examples: You are incapacitated for 5 minutes, you instead activate a different Effect counter to this one (i.e. Blast instead of Fascinate), you randomly teleport within 50 feet radius of your position (GM chooses the worst position), you cannot use other activated/targeted Effects for 1 hour, etc.*
  Detail required: Critical Failure effect

- **Disfiguring** [Passive | Stackable]
  Possession of this Gift grants a minor Battle Scar that can only be cured by buying off this Drawback.
  *Errata: E.g. chronic pain, wheezing, disfigurement, or a change in appearance that makes you obviously inhuman or similarly outlandish for settings where non-humans are common. See the stock Battle Scars on your character sheet for system references.*
  Detail required: Scar

- **Focus** [Passive]
  Using this Effect requires a specific kind of item.
  *Errata: Subject to Playgroup Leader approval. Drawing an accessible object (such as one in a holster) counts as a Quick Action (-2 dice), and removing something from a backpack or luggage requires a Full Action.*
  Detail required: Object

- **Maddening** [Power | Stackable]
  Possession of this Gift causes a Trauma that cannot be cured by any means. The chosen Trauma must be likely to come up on a Contract.
  *Errata: Buying off this Drawback cures the Trauma. The Trauma should require a Self-Control roll to avoid serious issues and loss of control. For example, "you must roll Self-Control to surface from any body of water," or "you must roll Self-Control to avoid jumping into fire when you see it," or "you cannot trust your life to someone else without rolling Self-Control." etc.*
  Detail required: Trauma

- **Specialist**
  Only attacks made by a specific type of object that is designed to be thrown (such as shurikens or throwing knives) will benefit from this Effect.
  *Errata: You cannot choose "objects not designed to be thrown."*
  Detail required: Type of thrown object

- **Unreliable** [Passive]
  When you activate this Effect, roll a single d10, Difficulty 4. If you fail or botch, the Effect fails and you cannot activate it again for an hour.
  *Errata: Any activation costs must still be paid.*
