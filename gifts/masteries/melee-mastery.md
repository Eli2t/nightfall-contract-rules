# Melee Mastery

*Master your weapon*

**Category:** Masteries

**Vectors:** Passive

**Modalities:** Power

## Description

This Power grants access to an array of effects improving your ability to fight with a chosen type of melee weapon.  It can also be used with the Unique Weapon Drawback to acquire a new weapon. In that case, the base stats for that weapon should be based off of an existing mundane melee weapon, and it should not behave much differently. 

Mastery powers are not obviously supernatural.

## Errata

As always, bonuses to the same dice pool do not stack. Instead, the highest bonus is used. This is also true of Damage, Armor, and Armor Piercing.

## Enhancements

- **Assassin**
  If your attack Injures your target, they cannot make a sound, even incidentally, for a single Round.
  *Errata: Even the sound of their body and equipment dropping to the ground is silenced.*

- **Called it**
  Called Shots that you attempt only require a Contested Outcome of 2 for small targets and 4 for tiny targets.

- **Dismember**
  If one of your Attacks causes a Battle Scar, you may select a Battle Scar one Severity higher as long as it is some form of dismemberment.

- **Falling Crane Strike**
  If you move and then make a successful attack against a target closer than the full distance you could have moved, you may choose to travel the excess movement in a straight line beyond the target.

- **Finesse**
  When Attacking a single being, you may limit the Severity of any Injury you inflict or determine a maximum Wound Level, including Incapacitated. The Injury is treated as normal for Battle Scars and Stabilization

- **Grip Discipline**
  You cannot be disarmed against your will by Minor, Major, or Severe Battle Scars, physical Actions, environmental conditions, botches, or Effects. Your weapon may still be destroyed.

- **Heavy Strikes**
  If you are attacking with a blunt weapon, if your attack causes an Injury you may choose to knock the target down and back 15 feet. Standing up is typically a Quick Action.

- **Hollywood Knockout**
  If you would successfully incapacitate a target, you may opt to knock them out for one hour instead of having them suffer an Injury.
  Requires: enhancements: finesse

- **Quick Draw**
  You may draw and equip yourself with any melee weapon on your person or within arm’s reach as a Free Action, as long as it is not being wielded by someone else.

- **Riposte** [Seasoned]
  If you successfully Defend against an attack within range of your melee weapon, you deal Damage equal to your excess Outcome plus Weapon Damage back to your attacker.

- **Sheathing the Sword**
  When you Clash 1v1 with an opponent, before the dice are rolled, you may have both sides deal Damage. If you do, your roll is made at -2 Difficulty, and you may use Will to Survive once this Round without affecting and regardless of its cooldown.

- **Two Swords are Better than One**
  If you are wielding more than one melee weapon, you Attack with all using the highest Weapon Damage, plus 1 for each additional weapon. Any Clash or Defend roll you make is -1 Difficulty, and mob penalties are ignored.
  *Errata: Maximum +5 Damage from bonus weapons. When you deal Damage via a Clash, you deal the highest weapon Damage without the multi weapon bonus.*

- **Whirlwind** [Seasoned]
  You may Exert your Mind to apply your next attack to any number of targets who are within melee range.
  *Errata: The activation cost may also be paid with Source.*

## Drawbacks

- **Conditional** [Passive | Stackable]
  This Effect is only available when a certain condition is met. This condition must be severe enough that this Gift is unusable the majority of the time.
  *Errata: The condition must be significant. Subject to GM approval.*
  Detail required: Condition

- **Critical Failure** [Passive]
  Roll a single D10 when you activate this Effect.  If you roll a 1, you critically fail in a way that is extraordinarily dangerous. Failure should cause at least a Severity-4 Injury, a Major Battle Scar, or an Effect counter to its purpose.
  *Errata: Other examples: You are incapacitated for 5 minutes, you instead activate a different Effect counter to this one (i.e. Blast instead of Fascinate), you randomly teleport within 50 feet radius of your position (GM chooses the worst position), you cannot use other activated/targeted Effects for 1 hour, etc.*
  Detail required: Critical Failure effect

- **Disfiguring** [Passive | Stackable]
  Possession of this Gift grants a minor Battle Scar that can only be cured by buying off this Drawback.
  *Errata: E.g. chronic pain, wheezing, disfigurement, or a change in appearance that makes you obviously inhuman or similarly outlandish for settings where non-humans are common. See the stock Battle Scars on your character sheet for system references.*
  Detail required: Scar

- **Maddening** [Power | Stackable]
  Possession of this Gift causes a Trauma that cannot be cured by any means. The chosen Trauma must be likely to come up on a Contract.
  *Errata: Buying off this Drawback cures the Trauma. The Trauma should require a Self-Control roll to avoid serious issues and loss of control. For example, "you must roll Self-Control to surface from any body of water," or "you must roll Self-Control to avoid jumping into fire when you see it," or "you cannot trust your life to someone else without rolling Self-Control." etc.*
  Detail required: Trauma

- **Mage**
  Activating this Effect requires two consecutive Actions of Concentration. It involves intricate use of the body and voice. If interrupted during activation, the Effect fails. It is obvious you are preparing to activate an Effect during casting.
  *Errata: Just about any attempt to interrupt the ritual should be successful.*
  Detail required: Activation Ritual

- **Specialist**
  These benefits only apply to a certain specific class of weaponry.
  *Errata: Examples: automatic rifles, six-shooters, knives, batons, Russian manufactured weapons, etc*
  Detail required: Type of Weapon

- **Unreliable** [Passive]
  When you activate this Effect, roll a single d10, Difficulty 4. If you fail or botch, the Effect fails and you cannot activate it again for an hour.
  *Errata: Any activation costs must still be paid.*
