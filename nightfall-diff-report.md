# Nightfall vs Official Rules — Diff Report

This report lists all mechanical differences between the Nightfall playgroup
rules and the official Contract RPG rules at thecontractrpg.com. Formatting and
wording differences are excluded.

Changes already documented in the Nightfall changelog are marked with
**(DOCUMENTED)** and grouped at the end of each section.

---

## Progression & Contractors

### Win Brackets — Granular Sub-Tiers
- **Official:** Five status tiers: Newbie (0-3), Novice (4-9), Seasoned (10-16), Professional (17-24), Veteran (25+). Contractors must be "within a 5-victory range."
- **Nightfall:** Ten sub-brackets: Newbie (0-3), Newbie-Novice (3-6), High Novice (6-9), Low Seasoned (9-12), Seasoned (12-15), High Seasoned (15-18), Vet Gate (19), Professional (20-23), Vet Test (24), Veteran (25+). Interaction limited to "within two win-brackets."

### Player Caps Per Bracket
- **Official:** No player cap beyond "at least two Players."
- **Nightfall:** 4 players at Newbie, 3 at High Novice, 2 at Seasoned, strictly solo at Vet Gate and beyond.

### Vet Gate / Vet Test Milestones
- **Official:** No concept of milestone gates at 19 or 24 wins.
- **Nightfall:** Win 19 is a tailored solo "Vet Gate Game." Win 24 is a "Vet Test." Winning the Vet Gate and each game past it unlocks one Veteran Gift Slot per win.

### Rework Milestones
- **Official:** No "Reworks" at status milestones.
- **Nightfall:** Novice Rework at 4 wins, Seasoned Rework at 10 wins, Vet-Gate Rework at 20 wins, Veteran Rework at 25 wins.

### Newbie Concept Malleability
- **Official:** No rule for freely changing Contractor concepts.
- **Nightfall:** Newbie Contractors' concepts are "malleable" and may change freely (e.g. Police PC becomes FBI Agent).

### Harbinger Transportation Cost
- **Official:** Harbingers provide free transport for Newbies.
- **Nightfall:** At Newbie-Novice+, Harbinger transport costs half a Contractor's Mind & Source (rounded up). Failure to arrive causes Declined Harbinger Invite and possible Loose Ends.

### Mandatory Combat Obstacle Scaling
- **Official:** No guidance on scaling combat difficulty by tier.
- **Nightfall:** Explicit benchmarks per bracket (Newbie = police team, Newbie-Novice = SWAT, High Novice = soldiers, Low Seasoned = special ops, etc.).

### Maximum Gift Value Formula
- **Official:** Total Gift Credits + Improvements <= 2 * Wins.
- **Nightfall:** Formula is (Wins * 2) + 1, with exceptions at 0 wins (max 3) and 1 win (max 4).

### **(DOCUMENTED)** Novice Gifts Require 4 Wins
### **(DOCUMENTED)** All PCs Start With a Free Gift
### **(DOCUMENTED)** Seasoned From The Start (win count / 3 formula, excluded combat gifts)

## Combat — Actions

### Called Shots — Thresholds Split by Melee/Ranged
- **Official:** Small = CO 4, Tiny = CO 6 (flat, no melee/ranged distinction).
- **Nightfall:** Small = CO 3 ranged / CO 2 melee. Tiny = CO 5 ranged / CO 4 melee.

### Called Shots — Head/Heart Bonus
- **Official:** Benefits at GM discretion.
- **Nightfall:** Head/heart called shots deal +2 damage, bypassing the Severity 5 cap.

### Called Shots — Extremity Battle Scar Escalation
- **Official:** No specific mechanic.
- **Nightfall:** Successful called shot to arm/leg deals a Battle Scar 1 level higher than usual.

### Called Shots — Gift Reduction Table
- **Official:** No codified interaction.
- **Nightfall:** With Gifts like Point Control: Small melee = 0 CO, Small ranged = 1, Tiny melee = 2, Tiny ranged = 3.

### Grappling — Choking Rework
- **Official:** Choking is a grapple at Difficulty 7.
- **Nightfall:** Choking is a Called Shot (Tiny target, CO 3 melee) at Difficulty 6. Separate "choke to kill" variant ignores material armor and deals +2 damage. Stacking severity injury system for sustained chokes.

### Grappling — Reaction Restrictions
- **Official:** Grappled targets cannot React to actions from outside sources.
- **Nightfall:** No such restriction. Instead, grappled targets suffer +1 Difficulty to attack grappler, +2 Difficulty to attack others.

### Grappling — Breaking Free via Clash
- **Official:** Breaking free uses Action only.
- **Nightfall:** Breaking free can use Action or Clash. Target may use Dexterity instead of Brawn for escape.

### Garrotte — Difficulty and Called Shot
- **Official:** Difficulty 8 (or 6 from behind).
- **Nightfall:** Difficulty 6, but requires Called Shot (Tiny target, CO 3). No sneak-up bonus.

### Disarms (New Mechanic)
- **Official:** No disarm rules.
- **Nightfall:** Full subsystem — Brawn/Dex + Melee as Action or Clash. Positive CO = take possession or launch item (CO * 5 feet). Can combine with Quick Action to equip.

### Shoves, Trips & Pins (New Mechanic)
- **Official:** No shove/trip/pin rules.
- **Nightfall:** Full subsystem — shove distance = CO * 2.5 feet. Can knock prone. Pinned (grappled + prone) = +2 Difficulty to attack grappler.

### Throws (New Mechanic)
- **Official:** No throw rules.
- **Nightfall:** Throw distance = CO * 5 feet. Weapon Damage = half target's Brawn. Thrown target also takes damage.

### Aiming — Expanded System
- **Official:** Flat -1 Difficulty for 1 Action of aiming while standing still.
- **Nightfall:** Requires Concentration. Stacking -X Difficulty per round. +2 Difficulty to awareness Perception. Must specify trigger. If target attacks, becomes a Clash.

### Weapon Handling Rules (New Mechanic)
- **Official:** No Brawn requirements for weapons.
- **Nightfall:** Brawn must match Weapon Damage or suffer +Difficulty equal to the difference. Gift-enhanced weapons use higher of base WD or half total (rounded up). Buster uses full WD.

### Targets in Melee — Expanded
- **Official:** Firearms into melee require a Called Shot, failure = half damage.
- **Nightfall:** Split by situation — vs 1 opponent = Small Called Shot (CO 3), vs Mob = Tiny Called Shot (CO 5). Adds high/low friendly-fire roll.

### Clashing with Firearms (New Subsystem)
- **Official:** No firearm-vs-firearm clash rules.
- **Nightfall:** Can Clash at range if gun is drawn. Winners shoot, losers forfeit Action. Multiple Clashes resolve in Initiative order.

### Full-Auto Sweep Area
- **Official:** Max 25x25 feet.
- **Nightfall:** Max 30x30 feet.

### Concentration — Perception Penalty
- **Official:** No Perception penalty while Concentrating.
- **Nightfall:** +2 Difficulty to Perception rolls for non-target awareness.

## Combat — Weapons & Armor

### Firearms — Handgun Range
- **Official:** 50 feet.
- **Nightfall:** 75 feet.

### Firearms — Massively Expanded Weapon List
- **Official:** 4 categories (Handgun, Shotgun, Rifles, Heavy Sniper).
- **Nightfall:** 19+ categories including Light Pistol, Handcannon, SMG, Auto Shotgun, Assault Rifle, Marksman Rifle, LMG, Anti-Tank Rifle, HMG, Rotary Cannon, Aircraft Cannon, plus Heavy Support Weapons (RPG, Guided Missile, Grenade Launcher, AGL, Flamethrower).

### Firearms — Sniper Handling
- **Official:** +3 without tripod or at melee range.
- **Nightfall:** +2 in melee, handling requirement (Brawn 4), +1 handheld past 300 feet, no aiming bonus handheld.

### Melee Weapons — Expanded
- **Official:** 7 categories.
- **Nightfall:** 9 categories adding Brass Knuckles (WD 0), Hammer (WD 2), Sledgehammer (WD 3). Thrusting Weapon allows Brawn or Dex.

### Non-Lethal Weapons — Reduced Difficulty
- **Official:** Handheld stun Diff 7, Ranged stun Diff 8, Mace Diff 8.
- **Nightfall:** Handheld stun Diff 6, Ranged stun Diff 6, Mace Diff 7. Uses Brawl instead of Melee.

### Non-Lethal Weapons — Armor vs Stun
- **Official:** Armor value adds to Body roll Outcome against stun.
- **Nightfall:** Armor does not add to Body roll.

### Armor — Called Shot Bypass System
- **Official:** No Called Shot bypass for armor.
- **Nightfall:** Every armor type specifies a Called Shot size required to circumvent (Tiny or Small).

### Armor — Historical Armor Types
- **Official:** No historical armor.
- **Nightfall:** Adds Full Plate, Scale, Chainmail, Breastplate, Gambeson — each with +2 vs slash/pierce and half armor vs firearms.

### Armor — Modern Tactical Tiers
- **Official:** Flak Jacket (Armor 3, Penalty 0).
- **Nightfall:** Replaces with FPV (Armor 4, Penalty 2), FAS (Armor 5, Penalty 3), Bomb Suit (Armor 6, Penalty 5).

### Shields — Expanded
- **Official:** 2 tiers (+1 and +3).
- **Nightfall:** 4 tiers (+1 through +4). Adds mobile shield rules. Bucklers allow using Brawl to Defend.

### Fragmentation Grenade
- **Official:** Within 5 feet = 15 dice damage.
- **Nightfall:** Direct hit = 10 flat damage + 2 armor penetration. Within 5 feet = 15 dice + 2 damage.

### Flashbang Difficulty
- **Official:** Body roll at Difficulty 6.
- **Nightfall:** Body roll at Difficulty 8.

### Land Mine Severity
- **Official:** Max Severity 4, Missing Leg.
- **Nightfall:** Max Severity 5, Missing Leg or Missing Foot.

### **(DOCUMENTED)** Stock Armor Nerfs (reinforced clothing -1 vs melee, kevlar 2, plate carrier 3 + 1 penalty)

## Combat — Movement & Vehicles

### Lifting Maximum — High Brawn
- **Official:** Brawn 6 = 500 lbs, Brawn 7 = 700 lbs, Brawn 8 = 1000 lbs.
- **Nightfall:** Brawn 6 = 450 lbs, Brawn 7 = 525 lbs, Brawn 8 = 600 lbs (strict linear formula).

### Pack Beast Encumbrance (New)
- **Official:** No pack beast rules.
- **Nightfall:** Full table for beast-assisted carrying (25 lbs per Brawn, 3x/5x multipliers).

### Mythic Brawn Lifting/Throwing Table (New)
- **Official:** No Mythic Brawn progression.
- **Nightfall:** Full table from Brawn 5-15 with throwable objects and Weapon Damage (3-13).

### High Speed Movement (New/Expanded)
- **Official:** Speed ratings 0-3 (max 500 ft/round).
- **Nightfall:** Speed ratings 0-8 (max 12,000 ft/round — Hypersonic). Adds high-speed dodging (speed rating as dodge floor at 4+) and crashing rules.

### Vehicle Body / Cumulative Damage (New)
- **Official:** No vehicle HP system; damage is component-based.
- **Nightfall:** Vehicles have Cumulative Body (min 12 or Armor * 2). Degradation at 50% and 33%.

### Vehicle Components — Tires
- **Official:** Any damage destroys non-run-flat tires.
- **Nightfall:** Tires have 1 Armor and 2 Body.

### Vehicle Armor — Tiered
- **Official:** Flat 10 Armor for all armored vehicles.
- **Nightfall:** Tiered: jury-rigged 6, APC 10, Tank 12+.

## Combat — Hazards

### Exposure Difficulty
- **Official:** Body roll at Difficulty 6.
- **Nightfall:** Body roll at Difficulty 8.

### Exposure — Exceed Cap Consequences
- **Official:** Minor Battle Scar or Severity 1 Injury.
- **Nightfall:** More granular — Botch at 3 Exposure = Disease/Condition. Failure = stacking Severity 1 Injury or minor Battle Scar. Difficulty increases per missing biological requirement.

### Deprivation — Codified Frequency
- **Official:** Intentionally vague, at GM discretion.
- **Nightfall:** Minimum 2 rolls per 24 hours.

### Thirst — Extreme Conditions
- **Official:** Hot/strenuous = 6 hours.
- **Nightfall:** Adds extreme tier (e.g. alien desert with two suns) = 3 hours.

### Damage Over Time — Stacking Severity
- **Official:** No explicit DOT tracking rule.
- **Nightfall:** DOT must always be tracked as a stacking severity injury.

### Difficulty Modifiers — Prone, Knocked Down, Grappled, Weapon Reach
- **Official:** None of these modifiers listed.
- **Nightfall:** Prone = +1 ranged / -1 melee. Knocked down = +1 melee. Grappled = +1/+2. Weapon reach = +1.

### Poisons/Toxins (New Subsystem)
- **Official:** No poison rules.
- **Nightfall:** Full system with 3 lethality tiers, dosing mechanics, onset time, Body resistance at Difficulty 8, medical treatment, and irritant rules.

### Drugs (New Subsystem)
- **Official:** No drug rules.
- **Nightfall:** Full system with addiction/dependence, onset times by delivery method, dosage levels, and specific subsystems for recreational drugs, stimulants, painkillers, and truth serums.

## Health & Recovery

### Body Table — Extended
- **Official:** Max shown is Brawn 7-8 = Body 9.
- **Nightfall:** Extends to Brawn 9-10 = Body 10, Brawn 11-12 = Body 11.

### Bodily Deterioration — Physical Conditions Count
- **Official:** Body reduced by 1 per Battle Scar after the 4th.
- **Nightfall:** Battle Scars AND Physical Conditions (Diseases/Disabilities) count. Body reduced to 0 = death.

### Mental Deterioration — Mental Conditions Count
- **Official:** Mind reduced by 1 per Trauma after the 4th.
- **Nightfall:** Traumas AND Mental Conditions (Disorders/Maddenings) count. Mind reduced to 0 = death.

### Disorders (New Category)
- **Official:** No separate "Disorders" category.
- **Nightfall:** New mechanical category distinct from Traumas, with unique interaction rules.

### Injuries — Severity 4+ Default to Battle Scar
- **Official:** Equipment destruction and Battle Scars are equal alternatives.
- **Nightfall:** Battle Scar is the default. Equipment destruction requires a separate GM-initiated coin flip.

### Injuries — Arcade Only
- **Official:** Two healing modes (Arcade and Gritty), Playgroup chooses.
- **Nightfall:** Arcade only. Gritty option removed.

### Abilities — Rank 6 Anti-Botch Missing
- **Official:** Rank 6 Abilities cannot have an Outcome lower than 0.
- **Nightfall:** No mention of the rank-6 anti-botch protection.

### Exertion — Source Replaces Mind for All Exertion
- **Official:** Source only replaces Mind for Gift activation.
- **Nightfall:** Source can replace Mind for any Exertion (except Mind Damage, failed Trauma rolls, or overriding Traumas/Disorders).

### Exertion — Override Traumas/Disorders
- **Official:** No universal override mechanic.
- **Nightfall:** Exert Mind (not Source) on a failure (not botch) to instantly succeed any Trauma/Disorder roll.

### Exertion — NPC Exertion Rules
- **Official:** No explicit NPC Exertion rules.
- **Nightfall:** Important/named NPCs may Exert. Superabled NPCs may always Exert.

### Source — Full at Start of Events
- **Official:** Regenerates at 1 per day only.
- **Nightfall:** Begin every Contract, Move, and Hustle with full Source.

### Mind — Full Reset at Contract/Downtime Start
- **Official:** Recovers at 1 per night of restful sleep only.
- **Nightfall:** Full Mind reset at start of each Contract and first Move of Downtime.

### Will to Survive — Extended to Downtimes
- **Official:** Once per Contract.
- **Nightfall:** Once per Contract AND once per Downtime.

### Recovery — GM Permission Required
- **Official:** No such restriction.
- **Nightfall:** Must get GM permission before recovering anything on your sheet.

### Recovery — No Idling for Resources
- **Official:** No such restriction.
- **Nightfall:** Cannot spend a Move entirely sleeping to recover resources.

### Downtime Injuries — Different Healing
- **Official (Arcade):** Downtime injuries count as received on the next Contract.
- **Nightfall:** Downtime injuries heal at 2-Severity rate at the start of the next Contract (partially healed before it begins).

### **(DOCUMENTED)** Samson Nerf (high/low roll on Will to Survive)
### **(DOCUMENTED)** Dice Rework (10 adds die, hidden non-combat rolls)

## Downtime & Contracts

### Passage of Time — OOC-Linked
- **Official:** About one month of in-game time per Contract.
- **Nightfall:** Downtime equals actual OOC time between Contracts (minimum one week). Total career spans ~4-6 months. Current date is always roughly "today."

### Move Duration Cap
- **Official:** No guidance.
- **Nightfall:** Moves should usually be capped to one week. Longer Moves split into multi-part recordings.

### Finances — Non-Cumulative + Overdraft
- **Official:** No overdraft mechanics.
- **Nightfall:** Finances reset per Contract cycle, are not cumulative. Overdraft drops Financial Levels temporarily. Past Poor = Debt Loose End.

### Loose Ends — Cutoff Visible to Players
- **Official:** Cutoff number hidden from Players.
- **Nightfall:** Players can see the Cutoff number OOC and intuitively IC.

### Loose Ends — Deadly Threat Definition
- **Official:** "Significant risk to Contractors or their Ambitions."
- **Nightfall:** "Significant risk even to Contractors." (Omits Ambition-level threats.)
