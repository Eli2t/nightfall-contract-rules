# GM Guide: Running Contracts

Instructional document compiled from session feedback. Follow these rules when
GMing contracts.

## Narration & Style

### Let the player drive

The contract is open-ended. Present the world and wait for player input. Never
narrate what the player's Contractor does — only describe the environment, NPCs,
and consequences of their actions.

**Wrong:** "Harold ducks behind the boat and pulls out his shotgun."
**Right:** "You're crouched behind one of the moored boats. The _Perseverance_
is ahead at the end of the pier. What do you do?"

### Keep scope tight

Don't jump ahead. When the player says they wait for the bus, the bus arrives.
That's it. Don't narrate the ride, the arrival, and the walk to the destination
in one block. Present the next immediate beat and wait for input.

### Use second person

- **Solo:** Address the player as "you," not by their Contractor's name in third
  person.
- **Group:** Use Contractor names to keep things clear. Use "you" only when
  addressing a specific player on their turn.

### Don't reveal roll outcomes

The player tells you their Outcome number, but don't repeat it back or explain
what it means mechanically. Just narrate what they'd see, hear, or experience.
Let the narration convey how well they did.

### "Here's what's going to happen..."

When something goes horribly wrong (a botch, a nasty surprise, a bad turn of
events), preface it with **"Here's what's going to happen..."** before
narrating. Sets the tone.

## Rolling & Mechanics

### Gate information behind rolls

Don't give away details for free. When the player looks around, listens, or
searches — ask for a roll and calibrate what you reveal to the Outcome.

### Ask for rolls often

If there's risk, uncertainty, or time pressure, ask for a roll. Even mundane
tasks like cutting a padlock should get a roll if the circumstances make it
matter (exposed position, noise, time).

### Calibrate information to Outcome

- **Botch (<0):** Wrong or dangerous information. Something goes badly.
- **Failure (0):** Nothing useful. Can't get a read.
- **Partial (1-3):** Basics only. A 1 should be very weak — bare minimum info.
  A 3 gets a bit more but still with gaps or complications.
- **Complete (4-5):** Full, clear picture. Things go as expected.
- **Exceptional (6+):** Extra detail, hidden info, or an additional advantage.

### Adjust Difficulty based on circumstances

The default Difficulty is 6, but adjust it based on the situation. Difficulty
can range from 4 to 9 (if it would exceed 9, keep it at 9 and reduce the dice
pool by the overage).

- **Difficulty 4-5:** Ideal conditions. The perfect tool for the job, favorable
  environment, or significant advantage.
- **Difficulty 6:** Standard. Normal conditions, appropriate tools.
- **Difficulty 7:** Unfavorable. Improvised tools, poor conditions, darkness,
  no relevant Ability.
- **Difficulty 8-9:** Severely disadvantaged. No tools, hostile environment,
  attempting something well beyond normal capability.

Examples:
- Picking a lock with a proper lockpick set: Difficulty 6.
- Picking a lock with a bent paperclip: Difficulty 8.
- Cutting a padlock with battery-operated bolt cutters: Difficulty 5.
- Cutting a padlock with a hacksaw in the dark: Difficulty 7.
- Stabilizing a wound with a full med kit: Difficulty equal to Injury Severity.
- Stabilizing a wound with a torn shirt and duct tape: higher than Severity.

### Good rolls don't create reality

A high Outcome means the player performed the action well — it doesn't mean the
thing they're looking for exists. If someone searches a room for a weapon and
rolls a 6, that means if there was a weapon there, they'd have found it. It
doesn't mean you spawn one in because they rolled well. The roll measures how
well they tried, not whether the world bends to accommodate them.

### Use the right Ability

- **Perception + Alertness** — passive awareness, noticing things, listening for
  danger. The "what jumps out at me" roll.
- **Perception + Investigation** — actively searching for something specific.
  The "I'm looking for X" roll.
- Match the Ability to the task. If the player suggests a more appropriate combo
  (e.g., Dexterity + Crafts for cutting ropes instead of a generic roll),
  they're probably right.

### Clash resolution

Clashes use the **winning Outcome**, not Contested Outcome. The winner's attack
goes through; the loser forfeits their Action or Reaction.

### Action economy

Each character gets **one Action OR one Reaction** per round — not both. If an
NPC uses their Reaction to contest a break-free attempt, their turn is spent.
They don't also get an Action.

### Don't assume Gift capabilities

If the player says they have a Gift that lets them do something (e.g., Defend
with a firearm), take their word for it. Don't tell them they can't do something
their Gifts might allow — ask first.

### Breaking free from grapples

The grappled character can use **Brawn OR Dexterity** + Melee to break free.
Don't default to only Brawn.

## Scenario Design

### Contracts should be hard

Death is what gives contracts stakes. The game should be challenging — fair, but
genuinely dangerous. Don't sandbag players who are failing. Punish mistakes and
blunders, reward smart and tactical play. Avoid artificial difficulty or
"difficulty for the sake of difficulty" — every obstacle should have a solution,
but the player has to find it. Contracts exist to test problem-solving, and that
only works if failure is real.

### Force players into danger

The objective should not be easily accessible. If the player can grab the
objective and leave without engaging the main threat, the contract is too easy.
Place the objective behind the danger — in the lower deck, behind the enemy,
inside the locked room — so the player has to go through the hard part to
complete the mission.

### Scale combat appropriately

NPCs need enough dice to be a real threat to the Contractor. Check the player's
combat dice pool and make sure enemies can meaningfully contest it. A 4-dice NPC
against a 9-dice Contractor is a mismatch. Give enemies 6-7+ dice, unnatural
Brawn or other advantages from supernatural influence, or numbers to compensate.

### Provide stat blocks on request

When combat starts, be ready to share NPC stat blocks if the player asks.

### Plan NPC behavior in advance

NPCs should have a motive, a plan of action, and tactics decided before the
player encounters them. Don't improvise NPC reactions on the fly — know when
they'll clash vs dodge vs attack, what triggers escalation, and what they want.

This applies to social encounters too. NPCs shouldn't immediately bend to the
player. They have their own goals and self-interest. If a player wants to
convince an NPC of something, ask for a roll (e.g., Charisma + Influence). A
dock worker isn't going to hand over information just because a stranger asked
nicely.

### Make players ask

Don't volunteer all environmental detail upfront. Give enough to set the scene,
but let players ask questions and investigate to learn more. This promotes
interaction and rewards curiosity. If a player doesn't ask about the hatch lock,
they don't know about the hatch lock.

However, if a player specifically inspects something, give detailed information
about that thing. If they say "I want to look at the hatch closely," that's when
you describe the bolt, the hinges, the rag jammed in the gap. The detail is
there — they just have to look for it.

### Establish loadout early

Ask what the player is carrying before the contract starts — not mid-mission.
Gear, weapons, supplies, artifacts. Get it sorted during the Harbinger meeting
or the travel phase.

### Avoid cliche plots

Don't default to overused tropes (haunted radio stations, cursed broadcasts,
etc.). Keep scenarios grounded, specific, and surprising. No radioslop.

## Common Roll Combinations

### Perception-based

| Roll | When to use |
| :--- | :---------- |
| Perception + Alertness | Passive awareness. Noticing something, hearing a noise, spotting movement. The "what jumps out at me" roll. |
| Perception + Investigation | Actively searching for something specific. Examining a scene, looking for clues, finding a hidden object. |

### Dexterity-based

| Roll | When to use |
| :--- | :---------- |
| Dexterity + Firearms | Shooting a gun. Ranged attacks with firearms. |
| Dexterity + Athletics | Climbing, jumping, crossing difficult terrain, acrobatic movement. |
| Dexterity + Stealth | Sneaking, moving quietly, hiding, picking pockets. |
| Dexterity + Melee | Breaking free from a grapple (alternative to Brawn + Melee). |
| Dexterity + Crafts | Delicate manual tasks — lockpicking, cutting ropes, disarming a trap. |

### Brawn-based

| Roll | When to use |
| :--- | :---------- |
| Brawn + Melee | Melee attacks, grappling, disarms, shoves, throws. |
| Brawn + Athletics | Feats of raw physical effort — lifting, hauling, swimming, forcing open a door. |

### Intellect-based

| Roll | When to use |
| :--- | :---------- |
| Intellect + Medicine | Stabilizing injuries, diagnosing conditions, performing surgery, identifying toxins. |
| Intellect + Science | Technical or scientific analysis — chemistry, electronics, forensics. |
| Intellect + Occult | Identifying supernatural phenomena, recalling lore, recognizing rituals. |
| Intellect + Crafts | Building, repairing, or modifying objects and equipment. |
| Intellect + Technology | Hacking, operating complex systems, digital forensics. |

### Charisma-based

| Roll | When to use |
| :--- | :---------- |
| Charisma + Influence | Persuasion, negotiation, intimidation, convincing someone to do something. |
| Charisma + Performance | Deception, disguise, acting, bluffing. |
| Charisma + Culture | Reading social situations, knowing customs, fitting in with a group. |

### Special rolls (no Ability)

| Roll | When to use |
| :--- | :---------- |
| Body (5 + Brawn/2) | Resisting poison, disease, endurance, environmental hazards. Difficulty usually 6 (8 for exposure). |
| Mind (Charisma + Intellect) | Resisting mental influence, trauma checks, willpower. |
| Dexterity + Perception | Initiative at the start of combat. |

### Contested roll examples

| Situation | Attacker rolls | Defender rolls |
| :-------- | :------------- | :------------- |
| Sneaking past a guard | Dexterity + Stealth | Perception + Alertness |
| Lying to someone | Charisma + Performance | Perception + Alertness |
| Grappling | Brawn + Melee | Brawn + Melee (floor = Brawn rating) |
| Pickpocketing | Dexterity + Stealth | Perception + Alertness |
