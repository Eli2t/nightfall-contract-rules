## Pyromancy

You can only set targets that are at least equal or smaller than your Fire Size,
on-fire--and you require oxygen (air) and fuel to do so at all.

### Fire Size Clarifications

- Torch (a part of the body is exposed to flame)

- Bonfire (half of the body is exposed to flame)

- Residential Fireplace = Raging inferno (entire body is engulfed in flame)

- Firepit's Fire = (A fire big enough to envelop a vehicle such as a civilian
  car.)

- Tree-sized Fire = (A fire big enough to encompass a large vehicle, such as a
  Tank, or Helicopter)

- Storage-shed sized Fire = (A fire big enough to envelop a small apartment)

If Legdermain has been taken; Blatant Thievery is now only a Quick Action but
may be attempted up to once a Round.

## Terrain Control

### Time Taken Per Attempt

Guidelines for Structure / Terrain Control Damage

In order to break a hole big enough to move through, you must break-through
utilizing Brawn (or similar) + a relevant ability with Difficulty depending on
the Equipment & tactics used.

A cumulative Outcome equal to the structure's Potency (or Armor for regular
structures / terrain) * 3, breaks a hole large enough for a person to move
through.

Any and all attempts take a default of (Potency * 5 Minutes) / (Outcome) to
perform, and carry out: such Active attempts are almost certainly exhausting &
requiring Concentration.

#### Tool Guidance

- 5 Brawn w/ no tools is rolled @ 9
- Generic weapons are rolled @ 7-8
- Appropriate manual tools are rolled @ 6.
- Power tools are rolled @ 4-5.
- Appropriate explosives are rolled @ 4, and only take as long as they require
  to detonate to actively attempt.

#### Examples

Trying to break a hole through a concrete wall with appropriate manual tools
requires a cumulative 24 Outcome. If you get an average Outcome of 5, every
attempt would take (8*5) / (5): 8 Minutes per attempt, up to about 40 Minutes
total, and be an incredibly exhausting & draining process just to break a hole
big enough to hunch through.

#### Repercussions

For certain structures, with improper methods / tactics / equipment: any
Botches will result in some sort of accident or short-term to long-term harm
to the one breaking out: such as a Severity 1 Injury. Failures cause a point
of Exhaustion Penalty.

## Upgrade

### Handling

When Upgrading a Weapon; the Weapon's new Handling Brawn is equal to the higher
of the base Handling or half of the new total Weapon Damage rounded up.

Examples:

- A 2 WDMG Pistol w/ +3 Upgrade would have a Handling of 3.
  - (2+3=5/2=2.5=3.)

- A 3 WDMG Shotgun w/ +3 Upgrade would have a Handling of 3.
  - (3+3=6/2=3)

### Enchant Enhancement

When using Enchant on a Firearm, or similar weaponry; "does not run out of fuel
or energy" extends to the ammunition. STANDARD Firearm systems would have
unlimited ammunition.

This does not extend to things like missile launchers, or most explosive small
arms.
