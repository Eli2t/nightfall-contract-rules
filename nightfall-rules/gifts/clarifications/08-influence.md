## Binding Oath

### Clarifications

Your Binding Oath does not extend to you powers that you do not already have /
are not attached as apart of the Oath's system.

You cannot "or anvils will fall on your head"--if you do not have an attached
Offensive Gift that... is an anvil that falls upon their head.

## Creature Possession

### Banned from Play

Creature Possession is an illegal Gift in Nightfall; Contractors cannot take
this Gift, ever, for any reason.

For possessing Creatures; use Possession.

## Possession

### "Intrinsic to Biology"

Gifts: You have access to and can use any of your own Powers which are not
intrinsic to your biology or physical body. You cannot access any Powers or
other supernatural effects which the target has, unless they are intrinsic to
the target's biology or physical body.

You do not get any of the target's memories, skills, or things they have learned
to do--you keep actual physical features / biological powers they have.

- You would keep...
  - A Vampire's fangs, and Regeneration (these are both passive, and linked to
    purely physiology.)

  - A Wendigo's cold resistance, and hardy fur.

- You would NOT keep...
  - A Vampire's ability to transform into mist, or amorphous forms, or any
    hemomatic abilities.

  - The Wendigo's ability to transform.

### Eviction

If you attempt to force your host to take actions which would violate one of
their Limits or their instinct for self-preservation, they may resist by rolling
Mind. If their Outcome is higher than or equal to the contested Outcome, the
possession ends and you are evicted from their body. Otherwise, they may Exert
their Mind to evict you from their body.

Long Term Resident (You cannot be evicted from the host for crossing their
Limits or putting them in danger if they fail their Mind resistance roll.) -

Making the target, even if you are Possessing them, do any form of Resistance
Roll (they still get to make it, regardless)--is violating their instinct for
self-preservation, and allows an Eviction Exert or an Eviction Roll.

Similarly; they get an Eviction Roll if any Effect that can be "Easily Resisted"
is made upon them--thus allowing an Eviction Exert / Roll.
