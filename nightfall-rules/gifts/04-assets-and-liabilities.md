# Assets and Liabilities
During
[Character Creation](../gameplay-basics/03-contractors.md)
Players may select various Assets and Liabilities to further customize their
Contractors. Assets grant beneficial Conditions and Circumstances, while
Liabilities grant negative Conditions, negative Circumstances, Battle Scars, and
Traumas.

All Assets and Liabilities have an Exp value. Taking an Asset costs its value in
Exp. In contrast, taking a Liability credits its value in additional Exp.

Assets and Liabilities are separated into three categories:

- Physical, which represent specific physical capabilities or defects such as
  being double-jointed, deaf, or missing a leg.

- Background, which represent aspects of the Contractor's backstory such as
  wealth, contacts in the criminal underworld, or having been raised by wolves.

- Mental and Emotional, which represent the Contractor's mental or emotional
  state such as the ability to rationalize their behavior, an overriding belief
  in the sanctity of life, a jaded outlook that leaves them resistant to
  emotional control, or an innate trust of others.

Additionally, some Assets and Liabilities are Restricted, meaning that they can
only be taken with the approval of your Playgroup Leader.

Most of the Conditions, Circumstances, Battle Scars, and Traumas granted by
Assets and Liabilities can also be acquired during Contracts and Downtimes.

# Changing Assets and Liabilities

## Via Downtime & Gameplay

The Battle Scars, Traumas, Conditions, and Circumstances granted by your
Contractor's Assets and Liabilities are not "protected." They may be lost,
mitigated, or changed by the events of Contracts and Downtimes. In such cases,
the Experience you have spent/received from taking the relevant Assets and
Liabilities does not change.

For example, let's say you start with the Rich Asset, which grants a
Circumstance that makes you rich. If you later get caught breaking a bunch of
laws or someone destroys the source of your wealth, then you may lose that
Circumstance. The Experience you spent on that Asset will not be refunded when
you lose it.

Similarly, if you start with the Liability One Eye, which grants a Battle Scar,
a fellow Contractor may use a Gift to heal that Battle Scar; but, you must spend
Experience to buy off that Battlescar because you took it as a Liability.
Otherwise; if not a Liability or Gift Drawback, that Contractor may use their
Gift to just heal your Battlescar.

All Liability Loose Ends will generally always tick down; making Moves against
them, or interacting with them, can never fully tie off a Loose End that you
took as a Liability and benefit from EXP from–they may only be “reset”,
prolonged, or otherwise.

If you want to buy off a Liability Loose End / fully tie one off; you have to
first tie it up in-world through a Move, then spend the EXP to do so.

## Via Editing your Character Sheet

Acquiring some Conditions and Circumstances don't demand the events of a
Contract or making a
[Move](../gameplay-basics/05-downtime.md); all they
require is time investment. In these cases, Playgroup Leaders may approve
Players to edit their Contractors' Assets and Liabilities after Character
Creation, allowing them to purchase them with Experience.

## Liability Limit in Nightfall

You may have no more than 5 total Liabilities in Nightfall.
(Example: You could have Weak Willed, Ugly, Group of Enemies, Vengeful &
Delusions of Grandeur.)

You have an insatiable urge to consume something unusual. (You no longer recover
Mind through restful sleep--instead, you recover 1PT of Mind per day by
consuming the subject of your hunger.)

- Examples.. (Must be difficult, dangerous, or complicated to get--these are

  - Faith (A person must, sincerely & genuinely, pray to you as if you were a
    deity w/ at least a 1 minute ritual.)

  - 100 KWH of Power (Thirty minutes to an hour at a city service transformer or
    powerline or similar)

  - Take this as a Supernatural Condition instead of The Hunger on-site

## 9 - Maddenings as Supernatural Conditions.

You may take a Maddening as a Supernatural Condition; when you do so it must
still be a Disorder or equivalent, but it loses the “You may exert, even on a
failure, to succeed or override” clause that all Traumas / Disorders inherently
possess in Nightfall.

These do not count towards your Maddening / Disfiguring Softcap, and grant you
EXP instead of a Gift Point.

Zoanthropy: You are possessed by an impulsive aspect that physically and

## 6 / 12 Illicit Supplier / Arsenal Clarification

Both these Assets mean you have the ability to readily access the restricted
items; it does not mean you can necessarily afford them. You are still required
to be able to afford them. You must be able to fiscally afford / somewhat
book-keep your Arsenals / Illicit Suppliers, ESPECIALLY if you are obtaining /
transporting large amounts of restricted items.

- "Large" is at the GM's discretion; but generally anything more than occasional
  additions to Stock / anything meant to be sold / delivered en-masse,
  qualifies.
  - Such as large amounts of illicit substances, explosives, firearms, etc.
    - If someone were attempting to, Arsenal or not, obtain & transport 30 RPGs
      & their warheads to rebel fighters in X country; this would certainly
      require some sort of Player agency.

- You may take an Illicit Supplier or Arsenal that satisfies your The Hunger, or
  similar fuel-based constituents.
  - "Blood (Blood bags, medical-grade amounts, blood of just about any non-Alien
    kind.)

- Note; just because you can actually legally transport such--does not mean you
  are legally allowed to license or sell them. YOU can obtain / reliably access
  & own them, not anyone else.

## 6 Imprisoned Clarification

Your Imprisonment, and the Circumstances around / pertaining to it, must fit the
following criteria. You must be in some form of Captivity that restricts your
resources, you must be being held in Captivity by Captors which ensure you
remain captive and deter / prevent you from escaping, and any Loose Ends that
are not capable of affecting you while in Captivity / due to your Captors
continue to tick down, but “pause” at a Cutoff of 2 Contracts until you escape.

- 1) You must have be in some sort of Captivity.
  - Your resources are restricted; you do not have access to much & must make a
    Move to obtain certain resources.
    - IE: You are on a jungle island, and have no signal, no access to money, no
      access to most Focuses, and no ability to effectively call for help.

- 2) You must be being held in Captivity by some sort of Captor(s).
  - There must be some sort of captor(s) that ensure that you remain captive.
    - IE: This jungle island is surrounded by hostile marine life, or perhaps
      cryptids or otherwise indigenous Zoans, who refuse to allow you to leave &
      will hunt you down if you attempt to do so.

- 3) Any Loose Ends that are not capable of affecting you due to your
  Imprisonment, will still tick down as normal, but will “Pause” at a Cutoff of
  2 Contract.
  - Meaning you’d be able to go on Next Contract a “free man”, and then after
    the Contract after that one; the Loose End procs if you do nothing about it.

## 12 Weak Willed Clarification

Weak Willed essentially means that anyone who attempts to mundanely socially
influence you at all, gains a mini-influence Effect against you--you roll to
"resist" this with Self Control @ flat Difficulty. (Ontop of +2 Difficulty to
resist actual influence / mental Effects.)

- This "mini-Gift" will not push you to violate your Limits, or obviously /
  blatantly suicidally endanger your own life

## 6 / 12 Group of Enemies / Individual Enemy Clarification

These don't ever end; you can make Moves against them to delay, or otherwise
"reset" the timer--but you can never end them. The Loose End will just be
different, and/or modified, depending on how your feud goes. Additionally: this
Loose End roughly scales with you.

- If your Group of Enemies is a Mahobatsu Sorcery Clan-Corporation; you will be
  ganked up the Grade scale pretty much.

## Anti-Negation Clause

You cannot benefit from a Liability that you actively & obviously negate at all
times, and benefit from having completely at all times;

- You cannot take Hemophilia and Regeneration.

- Missing Arm and Extra Appendage is fine, if it is an Artifact or similar.

## How to Implement

Note: This is done by going to Edit Contractor, and using a Secondary Ability to
represent the EXP Expenditure, and then going to the sheet itself, and
copy-pasting your House Asset.

Verbal Instructions

1)

Locate the Playgroup Asset you are searching for; once you have found it, tab
it.

2)

Match its EXP Cost with a Secondary skill; label the Secondary Skill (EXP Cost)
Asset Name w/ the description that either labels it as the asset, or describes
what it is or does.

3)

Copy the Asset from the channel, to the sheet through Add Circumstance or Add
Condition--neither of which are in Edit Contractor but rather are just on the
sheet if you scroll down.

4)

You're done.

Note: The Secondary Ability used to represent the EXP Expenditure does not grant
you any bonus other than the Asset itself, and MUST BE LABELED [EXP COST] -
[ASSET NAME]--you have to do this.

- Example: 8 - Recoil Ready Asset

Note: You can only implement Custom Assets as they are, from the approved lists
below. (edited)
