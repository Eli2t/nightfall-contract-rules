# General Gift Rules

This section contains general reworks for Gifts / Gift-Adjacents that are
present on a broad spectrum.

## Duration in Months = Downtime

Any Gift that says its duration lasts "a Month" instead reads as "Downtimes" for
Nightfall.

## All PCs start with a free Gift

All Contractors in Nightfall begin play with a free Gift Point; the website will
flag you with a “red mark” for this, but you’re good to just ignore it.

This means that the maximum Gift Value one can start with is 3 (Free Gift Point,
Gifted Asset, and Charon Coin).

- The maximum in terms of Gift Value a Contractor can have at any given win
  range is the following. (From Improvements, winning games, etc)

- The general formula is (Wins * 2) + 1.
  - The only exceptions are 0, and 1 win.

  - 0 = 3

  - 1 = 4

  - 2 = 5

  - 3 = 7

  - 4 = 9

  - 5 = 11

  - So on, so forth

## Seasoned From The Start

Contractors in nightfall are able to take seasoned gifts equal to your win count
divided by 3. Eg:

- 0-2 Wins
  - 0
- 3-5 Win
  - 1
- 6-8 Wins
  - 2
- 9 Wins
  - 3
- 10+ Wins
  - Seasoned as standard

Combat seasoned effects (Weapon Masteries, Artifact Weapons, and gifts under the “Offensive” category [with the exception of environment control]), as well as a few additional gifts (listed below) are excluded from this, and require 10 wins as standard. The full list of excluded gifts are as follows:

- Weapon Masteries
  - Unarmed Mastery
  - Throwing Mastery
  - Archery Mastery
  - Firearm Mastery
  - Melee Mastery
- Artifact Weapons
  - Thrown Weapon
  - Special Firearm
  - Projectile Launcher
  - Melee Weapon
- Offensive Gifts
  - Traumatize
  - Spawn Minions
  - Injure
  - Blast
  - Afflict
- Other combat-related gifts
  - Armor
  - Pyromancy
  - Regeneration
  - Alternate Form

## Disfigurings / Maddenings

Contractors in Nightfall are limited to a cumulative amount of Disfigurings or
Maddenings depending on the bracket they are in.

Cumulative means that if you were in Newbie-Novice (3-6); you would have a
cumulative soft-cap of 3, meaning you could take 2 Maddenings and One
Disfiguring, or two Disfigurings and one Maddening, or Three Maddenings / Three
Disfigurings, total.

Disfigurings / Maddenings obtained from crafted Artifacts, other than your own,
do not count towards this.

Maddenings must be on the same level as a Disorder; and lose any “Exert to
succeed, even on a failure” clauses. The chosen Trauma must be likely to come up
during a Contract.

Disfigurings must be at least a major battle scar or higher, or a minor scar
that would pose a significant downside to your specific build.

## Crafting Softcap

Contractors in Nightfall, prior to completing their Novice Rework (4 wins), are
“soft capped” to the following, per Crafting Gift. This is lifted once a
Contractor finishes their Novice Rework.

- Consumables
  - The Contractor may hold no more than 9.

- Crafted Artifacts
  - The Contractor may craft no more than 4.

## Resistible is resistible.

Certain Gifts can be “Easily Resisted”; on-site this reads as (The target must
either consent to be targeted or be unconscious, hypnotized, thoroughly drugged,
in surgery or similarly incapacitated such that they could not or would not roll
to resist this Power.) and includes a ? errata that (If the condition is
satisfied, no Defensive Rolls may be made by the target).

In Nightfall; any and all Easily Resistible / Effects that say The target can
easily Resist are merely only usable if the target has satisfied the above
conditions,

Additionally, if the target would want to make a Resistance Roll, they may make
a Resistance Roll @ Difficulty 6. If the Effect originally required a
resistance roll at higher Difficulty; they roll that instead.

Note: depending on the Gift, compelling a target to choose to not make a
Resistance Roll against an Easily Resistible Effect–does not deny them their
roll.

- IE: Creature Transformation w/ Join The Herd
  - Just because you convince a Kaijuu somehow to be transformed into a mouse so
    you can squish them–does not mean they actually transform into a mouse.

## Focus / Fuel Specificity

A Focus must be under two levels of specification (A type of Item) -> (A
category of items within that type).
Examples: (Swords -> Rapiers) (Firearms -> Revolvers) (Religious Ornaments
-> A Crucifix) (Fancy Clothes -> A Suit Overcoat)

Focuses must always require one or more of the following to be valid; must
always require some form of significant interaction, must be problematic and/or
difficult to transport, must pose some sort of drawback beyond merely needing
the item itself.

Fuels likewise require two levels of specification and must meet the same
requirements as focuses.

- Examples of valid Focuses.
  - A person's skull.

  - A specific weapon category...
    - Revolver

    - Claymore

    - Katana

    - Rapier

    - Scythe

  - A specific religious ornament / tool.
    - A Mala

    - A Rosary.

    - A Crucifix

    - Focuses are always, at at least Difficulty 9, to create through Fabricate,
      or similar Effects.

  - A specific type of clothing.
    - Ceremonial shrine garb.

    - Traditional Orthodox Robes.

    - A three-piece suit.

- Example of valid Fuels
  - A Pint of Blood of a Specific Type (standard unit for a blood bag)

  - The corpse of a person or specific creature.
    - Such as a dog, a child, an ape, a wolf, a werewolf, an alien monster, etc.

  - A [Insert Religion] talisman.
    - IE: A Taoist Talisman
      - Fulu Talismans.

      - Curse Tags

    - IE: A Christian Talisman
      - Such as a bible.

      - A metal crucifix.

      - Holy water.

    - IE: A Voodoo Talisman.
      - Shrunken Heads

      - Gris-Gris

  - Specific objects of significant sentimental value.
    - The last gift given by one’s dead wife.

    - A wedding ring.

    - A disabled veteran’s uniform.

  - 10 kWh of power. (A large lithium ion battery, a very large power bank, an
    enormous amount of smartphones or laptops, 10 car batteries, etc )

Note: the above bullet points are clarifications; “A Taoist Talisman” is valid
for Fuel, as is “An Object of Sentimental Value”--the lists beneath them are
clarificatory.
