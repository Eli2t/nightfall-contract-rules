# Stock Delusions

Stock Delusion write-in

- You must succeed a Self-Control roll in order to not believe your delusion,
  even when confronted with compelling evidence, or in order to act in a way
  that is contrary to your delusion.

Delusion of Persecution

- You must succeed a Self-Control roll in order to not believe that others are
  conspiring against you or trying to harm you, even when presented with
  evidence showing otherwise.

Delusion of Invincibility

- You must succeed a Self-Control roll in order to not believe that you are
  invincible or immune to harm, even when presented with evidence that
  demonstrates the risks or dangers you face.

Delusion of So-Many-Supes

- You must succeed a Self-Control roll in order to not believe that anyone or
  everyone that you encounter, in fact, is Superabled or otherwise of a
  Supernatural paradigm.

Delusion of Identity

- You must succeed a Self-Control roll in order to not believe that you are
  someone else entirely (e.g., a famous person or historical figure), even when
  presented with evidence proving otherwise.

Delusion of Wealth

- You must succeed a Self-Control roll in order to not believe that you are
  extremely wealthy or financially secure, even when faced with evidence showing
  a lack of financial resources.

Delusion of Being Under Surveillance

- You must succeed a Self-Control roll in order to not believe that you are
  being constantly watched or monitored by external forces, even when confronted
  with evidence showing otherwise.

Delusion of Illness

- You must succeed a Self-Control roll in order to not believe that you have a
  serious or terminal illness, even when presented with medical evidence
  disproving the existence of such a condition.

# Stock Compulsions

Stock Compulsion Write-In

- When faced with a new opportunity to act on your compulsion, you must succeed
  in a Self-Control roll to resist.

Compulsion to Clean

- When faced with an opportunity to clean or organize your environment, you must
  succeed in a Self-Control roll to resist acting on this compulsion.

Compulsion to Hoard

- When faced with an opportunity to acquire or keep items, especially those that
  others might discard, you must succeed a Self-Control roll to resist acting on
  this compulsion.

Compulsion to Check

- When faced with an opportunity to verify or double-check something (e.g.,
  locked doors, appliances), you must succeed a Self-Control roll to resist
  acting on this compulsion.

Compulsion to Count

- When faced with an opportunity to count items or keep track of numbers, you
  must succeed in a Self-Control roll to resist acting on this compulsion.

Compulsion to Reassure

- When faced with an opportunity to seek reassurance or validation from others,
  you must succeed in a Self-Control roll to resist acting on this compulsion.

Compulsion to Avoid

- When faced with an opportunity to avoid certain situations or people that you
  find distressing, you must succeed in a Self-Control roll to resist acting on
  this compulsion.

Compulsion to Symmetrize

- When faced with an opportunity to arrange items in a symmetrical or orderly
  fashion, you must succeed in a Self-Control roll to resist acting on this
  compulsion.

Compulsion to Collect

- When faced with an opportunity to collect items related to a particular
  interest or obsession, you must succeed in a Self-Control roll to resist
  acting on this compulsion.

Compulsion to Apologize

- When faced with an opportunity to apologize or make amends, even for minor or
  imagined transgressions, you must succeed a Self-Control roll to resist acting
  on this compulsion.

# Stock Disorders

Clinically Insane

- Regardless of your actual intellect or mental faculties; you are insane, and
  you frequently act like it. (Your Mind Rating is 2 levels lower than it should
  be; represented on the sheet by permanent & unhealable Mind Damage. You suffer
  +1 Difficulty on any & all Mind Rolls)

Borderline Personality Disorder (BPD)

- You cannot handle rejection, being called out, being unimportant, being
  abandoned or otherwise feeling “unstable” & unsupported. Anytime you are made
  to feel / experience any of the prior, roll Self Control. If you fail you will
  begin acting impulsively, reactively & often extremely to avoid such feelings
  & circumstances which cause them, often exacerbating your own condition; these
  “splits” are often violent, physically or otherwise.
  - IE: Splitting up because you can’t talk to an important person & your
    colleague needs to do it alone; you roll Self Control, you fail & you have
    an outburst if you can’t get your way.

Antisocial Personality Disorder (ASPD)

- While you may have learned so, you inherently lack some or all capacity for
  empathy or regard for others, the law & other people at-large. You must make a
  Self Control roll to show any sort of kindness, compassion, empathy,
  consideration or otherwise care for another; on a failure, you will disregard
  or dismiss the needs, wellbeing, or feelings of those around you.
  - IE: Performing first aid on someone, using a healing Gift, or otherwise
    being helpful to someone.

  - IE: Listening to someone’s feelings, or acting in a way that takes that into
    account.
    - Similarly, playing “Good Cop” or trying to pretend to be a good person to
      socialize.

    - IE: Caring about the wellbeing of others; like buying other people
      jackets, or allowing them to have the only parachute out of the plane.

Psychopathy

- You do not tolerate Loose Ends, you leave no stone unturned, you are cold,
  calculating & callous & you absolutely will take everything you can, whenever
  you can. Whenever faced with major opportunities or vulnerabilities in which
  you could exploit, manipulate or harm someone else for personal gain, or
  otherwise to save yourself major inconvenience, you must succeed in a Self
  Control roll to avoid doing so. When you act on your tendencies; you must
  callously & aggressively do so–no softballing.
  - IE: Allowing a small child who witnessed you committing a crime, to get away
    alive.

  - IE: Leaving any witnesses to your crime… any

  - IE: Giving back something that you borrowed, when you could otherwise just
    keep it and walk.

  - IE: Letting someone live.

Schizophrenia

- You hallucinate frequent auditory & visual distortions which detrimentally
  affect your decision making & ability to discern what’s real & what isn’t
  sometime. Whenever you are performing a task in which perception or
  dissertation is prominent or primarily important; roll Self Control, on a
  failure your hallucinations cause you to fail the roll, as you get distracted,
  driven away, or otherwise “caught up” in fabrications of “false reality”.
  Dismissing “false realities” require significant interaction, detrimental
  distraction or repeat rolls. You are at +2 Difficulty to any roll made against
  Illusions or Illusory effects.
  - IE: You're supposed to do watch duty, you roll Perception + Alertness but
    then you fail your Self Control Roll… the GM gets to make up whatever
    bullshit they want to tell you & you have to gauge what actually is real.
    - GMs may even invent entirely fake or fabricated NPCs or storylines or
      plot-threads as a part of this.

Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD)

- You have a “right way” you want & “need” things to be; any time you majorly
  encounter uncleanliness, untidiness, unorganization, messes, or otherwise are
  forced to deal with the unroutine or unstructured, make a Self Control Roll.
  If you fail; you will burn an unreasonable amount of time “stunlocked” in your
  compulsion–taking (or wasting) at least 2x as much time as you would have
  otherwise.
  - IE: You really need to get this rental car to the other Contractors… but you
    don’t like the way the seats look & you need to arrange this car in your own
    personal manner.

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD)

- Whenever you are faced with triggers that remind you of past traumatic
  situations, such as loud yelling, particularly bad fights, explosive or loud
  pops, or similar; roll Self Control. If you fail, you shut down, flee, act
  impulsively or switch into “survival” mode as you take on some sort of
  combative (often violent) reaction.
  - IE: Upping a gun at teenagers for throwing pop rocks at your feet.

Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)

- You are incredibly fearful & anxious; when faced with the Disturbing, the
  scary, the horrible or terrible or otherwise anything which could feasibly
  threaten your life or livelihood; roll Self Control. If you fail, you freeze
  up, withdraw, become incredibly defensive, comply, or otherwise become
  avoidant to detrimental degrees.
  - IE: You encounter a monster; roll to not run away or freeze up & get
    attacked.

  - IE: You encounter an alien; roll to not run away in fear that it’ll probe
    you.

Bipolar Disorder

- You are especially susceptible to elevated emotions & in especially tense or
  stressful situations you must roll Self Control in order to regulate yourself
  & avoid taking extreme actions driven by your elevating (or elevated)
  emotional mood states. If you fail; you will engage in extreme actions &/or
  outbursts inspired by prior elevated emotion. You are at +2 Difficulty to
  resist any Effect whose primary purpose is controlling your Emotions.
  - IE: You are getting arrested by police, or about to; roll to not panic
    attack mode & run away, or at them.

Narcissistic Personality Disorder (NPD)

- Whenever you are humbled, made to act humble, otherwise must pretend to be
  humble, or are faced with major opportunities to seek, pry or manipulate
  others for personal gain or prestige; make a Self Control Roll. Should you
  fail, you seek out & pry admiration / esteem from your surroundings; even if
  it takes force or fear.
  - IE: The VIP (who it behooves you to be amicable to) is being a dick, roll to
    not forcefully extract gain / self esteem from them; through fear or force.
    - IE: Tearing them apart socially, or beating the shit out of them.

Histrionic Personality Disorder

- When faced with major opportunities to seek attention or engage in dramatic
  behavior, you must succeed a Self-Control roll to control these impulses and
  act more appropriately--should you fail, you will perform some sort of
  attention-grabbing attention-seeking dramatic & disruptive display–often, to
  your detriment.
  - IE: You enter a crowded space, such as a theater, a grocery store, a public
    plaza, or a school classroom, for the first time (or are otherwise around a
    crowd of people). If you fail; you’d immediately do something to grab their
    attention, like start screaming and dancing.
