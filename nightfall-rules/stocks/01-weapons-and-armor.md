# Weapons and Armor

## Firearms

Firearms are quite powerful, but they can be difficult to transport and obtain.

The effective range listed below is not a maximum range. Guns may be fired at
targets beyond that, with the Difficulty increasing by +1 for each additional
increment.

Attacks made at all are "All-Out Attacks"--for Firearms, this means that the
amount of rounds expended during an Attack is abstracted.

For "single-shot" / ammunition-saving fire modes outside of the abstracted
Attack: halve Weapon Damage and round down.

Reloading is always at least an Action, some Firearms may require multiple
Actions to reload (such as box / belt-fed Machine Guns, or heavy duty explosive
weapons).

- **Weapon Damage:** 6, and penetrates 4 Armor
- **Effective Range:** 7500 feet

### Aircraft Cannon
*Typically mounted on an Aircraft.) (Modified GAU-8 30mm & similar*
- **Attack:** Difficulty 6, +2 in Melee Range. Requires 3 additional levels of Handling than usual (9)--users may ignore the additional handling stipend if the weapon has proper support. Proper support, such as a bipod or tripod, always takes at least two Quick Actions to deploy and setup. May Full-Auto Sweep up to a 30x30 area as an attack. If somehow wielded “handheld”, these weapons always fire with at least +2 Difficulty.
- **Weapon Damage:** 6, and penetrates 4 Armor
- **Effective Range:** 7500 feet.
- **Reload:** 2 attacks Every attack is a full auto sweep that covers a 50x50 area. Reloading generally takes a dedicated crew about 10 minutes.

**Note:** "Proper Support" refers to things like a bipod, a tripod, a vehicle
fixture, structure fixture, prone on stable ground, etc.
The two quick actions are an abstraction for deploying the bipod, and then
supporting the weapon with–and other combinations. GMs may call for making a
roll on "unstable fixtures" or "unstable terrain" at their discretion.

Heavy weaponry mounted onto vehicles, typically, hand waves any Handling Rules.

Note that tracking reloads and ammo is an optional rule at the GM's discretion.
The "reload after X attacks" rating above reflects that Contractors likely fire
more than once during a given all-out attack.

## Heavy Support Weapons

Heavy Support Weapons are weapons that utilize similar rules to firearms but
fire, instead of a traditional round / bullet, an explosive ordnance such as a
rocket, missile or grenade.

### Rocket Launcher / “RPG”
- **Attack:** Difficulty 8 (-2 against most Vehicles) Outcome determines how far from the target you hit. 25ft - (5ft * Contested Outcome) = Distance. 0ft = Direct Hit.
- **Damage & Effect:** 15@6 + 2 (Splash Damage). Damage decays by 1 per 5 ft away, stopping completely at 50ft. If a direct hit, deals 12 Damage, penetrates 3 Armor & splashes targets within the radius.
- **Effective Range:** 600 feet
- **Reload:** Takes 2 Rounds to Reload after a single attack.

### Guided Missile Launcher
- **Attack:** Roll Dex + Firearms @ 8 (-2 against most Vehicles); a (1-3) Partial Success requires 2 Actions & Concentration, a (4-5) Full Success only requires 1. Declare a Called Shot prior to firing, after firing; roll 15@5 as the Missile “tracks” & guides itself to the target. If Direct-Firing, instead just use RPG rules (Difficulty 8.) Outcome determines how far from the target you hit. 25ft - (5ft * Contested Outcome) = Distance. 0ft = Direct Hit.
- **Damage & Effect:** 15@6 + 2 (Splash Damage). Damage decays by 1 per 5 ft away, stopping completely at 50ft. If a direct hit, deals 12 Damage, penetrates 3 Armor & splashes targets within the radius.
- **Effective Range:** 3000 feet
- **Reload:** Takes 1 minute to Reload after a single attack.

### Grenade Launcher
- **Attack:** Difficulty 6 Outcome determines how far from the target you hit. 25ft - (5ft * Contested Outcome) = Distance. 0ft = Direct Hit. If firing multiple Grenades, +2 Difficulty Grenades explode on-impact, but may still be reacted to as normal.
- **Damage & Effect:** 15@6 + 2 (Splash Damage). Damage decays by 1 per 5 ft away, stopping completely at 50ft. If a direct hit, deals 10 Damage & penetrates 2 Armor & splashes others. If firing multiple Grenades, increase the damage by 1 per additional grenade fired regardless of a direct hit, or splash (Up to a maximum of 5 per attack).
- **Effective Range:** 400 feet
- **Reload:** For Single-Shot Launchers, takes an Action to reload after a single attack. For Multi-Shot Launchers, takes an Action to reload per shot.

### AGL (Automatic Grenade Launcher) Typically mounted on a Vehicle.
- **Attack:** Difficulty 6 Outcome determines how far from the target you hit. 25ft - (5ft * Contested Outcome) = Distance. 0ft = Direct Hit. Grenades explode on-impact, but may still be reacted to as normal.
- **Damage & Effect:** 15@6 + 2-7 (Splash Damage). Damage decays by 1 per 5 ft away, stopping completely at 50ft. If a direct hit, deals 10-15 Damage & penetrates 2 Armor & splashes others. Fires a burst of up to 5 Grenades “per attack”; up to +5 Damage per attack.
- **Effective Range:** 400 feet
- **Reload:** Each attack is typically a 5-round Burst of Grenades; usually Reloads in 6 Attacks & takes up to a minute to Reload.

### Flamethrower
- **Attack:** Difficulty 6 May Full-Auto Sweep up to a 30x30 area w/ +1 Difficulty as an attack; expends 3 Rounds of Reload.
- **Damage & Effect:** Contested Outcome determines the initial “Burn Damage”, Any mundane material Armor (Kevlar, Clothing, etc) blocks the first instance of damage, but is depleted by it, destroyed if it reaches 0. Targets hit are set ablaze; taking 3 damage at the start of the flamethrower’s turn, starting next round. Targets may extinguish themselves by rolling Dex + Athletics @8 & achieving at least 3 Successes, or through the usage of extinguishants. Both the initial burn damage & subsequent “fire” injuries are tracked as once Stacking Severity Injury.
- **Effective Range:** 30 Feet
- **Reload:** 3 attacks(or 1 Full Auto Sweep) Takes roughly 5 minutes to reload a Flame Thrower.

## Explosives

**GM Tip:**

GMs should always give Contractors a roll to detect explosive traps, no matter
how well-hidden they are. Dying in one shot to an undetectable trap is never a
good experience.

### Stick of Dynamite (TNT)
*0.5 lbs*
- **Delay:** Fuse (variable)
- **System:** Targets within 5 feet take 10 Damage, outside radius take 10 dice of damage, decays by 5 dice per 5 feet out
- **Notes:** Effective throwing range of 50 feet. Semi-volatile, risk if stored improperly

### Land Mine
*2 lbs*
- **Delay:** Pressure Trigger
- **System:** Targets within 5 feet take 4d10 + 4 Damage.
- **Notes:** Land mines may inflict an Injury of maximum Severity 5. When they do so, those affected receive either Battle Scar: Missing Leg, or Missing Foot.

### Brick of C-4
*1.25 lbs*
- **Delay:** Detonator (variable)
- **System:** Targets within 5 feet take 15 damage, outside radius take 15 dice of damage, decays by 5 dice per 5 feet out
- **Notes:** M112 demolition block, US military

### Satchel Charge, WWII
*10 lbs*
- **Delay:** Detonator (variable)
- **System:** Targets within 15 feet take 15 damage, outside radius take 15 dice of damage, decays by 5 dice per 15 feet out
- **Notes:** WWII era, US Army M37 Demolition Kit

### Satchel Charge, modern
*20 lbs*
- **Delay:** Detonator (variable)
- **System:** Targets within 20 feet take 15 damage, outside radius take 15 dice of damage, decays by 5 dice per 20 feet out
- **Notes:** US Army M183 Demolition Charge Assembly

### Small IED (letter bomb)
*10 lbs*
- **Delay:** Fuse / Detonator
- **System:** Targets within 5 feet take 15 damage, outside radius take 15 dice of damage, decays by 5 dice per 5 feet out
- **Notes:** Volatile, high risk of accident

### Medium IED (package / container)
*100 lbs*
- **Delay:** Fuse / Detonator
- **System:** Targets within 20 feet take 15 damage, outside radius take 15 dice of damage, decays by 5 dice per 20 feet out
- **Notes:** Volatile, high risk of accident

### Large IED (car bomb)
*1000 lbs*
- **Delay:** Fuse / Detonator
- **System:** Targets within 65 feet take 15 damage, outside radius take 15 dice of damage, decays by 5 dice per 65 feet out Note: This is not just any car that explodes, this is a car that has been filled with explosive ordnance.
- **Notes:** Volatile, high risk of accident

## Grenades

All grenades have a delay of 2 Rounds, meaning they explode on your turn the
Round after you throw them. They have an effective throwing range of 100 feet.
Grenades weigh 2 pounds.

### Fragmentation
- **System:** Targets directly hit take 10 damage w/ 2 points of Armor Penetrated. Targets within 5 feet take 15 dice of Damage +2, decaying by 1 Damage per 5 feet out, stopping entirely at 50 feet

### Smoke
- **System:** Smoke grenades create a smokescreen with a radius of 0 feet upon detonation. Every 2 Rounds thereafter, the radius is increased by 5 feet, to a maximum of 25 feet. Smokescreens cause effective blindness for those caught inside as long as they are inside, and cannot be seen through. They last 2 minutes in a poorly-ventilated room or 5 Rounds when outside. Smoke grenades are not effective in actively-ventilated rooms or windy environments.

### Tear Gas
- **System:** As per smoke Grenades but start with a 5 foot radius and use Irritant rules instead of blindness.

### Flashbang
- **System:** Radius 20 feet. Make a Body resistance roll at Difficulty 8. Everyone within that range suffers Physical Stress increased by 7 - Outcome, decaying one level per Round. Affected targets are deafened for 30 seconds. If inside or in a dark area, affected targets are blinded for one Round and suffer a -3 Penalty to any vision-based perception rolls for 5 Rounds.

**Cooking off Grenades:**
You may "cook off" a grenade by holding it for a Round and then throwing with a
Perception + Athletics roll, Difficulty 8. Failure or partial success
indicates a dangerous throw. If you do so, it goes off the Round you throw it.
Fully cooked-off grenades may still be Reacted to.

## Non-Lethal Weapons

### Handheld Stun Gun
- **Attack:** Dexterity + Melee, Difficulty 6
- **Maximum Range:** 5 feet
- **System:** If the attack deals any damage, the target must roll Body @ Difficulty 9. If they succeed, they suffer a temporary dice penalty of 4 - Outcome on their next Action. If they fail, they are stunned & lose their next Action. Otherwise, if they botch, they are stunned and their “Tazer” grows by 1 Severity.

### Ranged Stun Gun
- **Attack:** Dexterity + Firearms, Difficulty 6
- **Maximum Range:** 30 feet
- **System:** If the attack deals any damage, the target must roll Body @ Difficulty 9. If they succeed, they suffer a temporary dice penalty of 4 - Outcome on their next Action. If they fail, they are stunned & lose their next Action. Otherwise, if they botch, they are stunned and their “Tazer” grows by 1 Severity. The target must re-roll Body for the next 3 Rounds. These Body rolls can end early if the darts are removed or if they roll an Outcome of at least 4.

### Mace, Pepper-spray
- **Attack:** Dexterity + Melee, Firearms, or Athletics, Difficulty 7
- **Maximum Range:** 10 feet
- **System:** If the attack hits, the target is blinded for 10 Rounds (30 seconds). Blinded targets automatically fail any roll requiring sight and suffer up to a -3 dice penalty to any roll that would benefit from sight. A sight penalty of -2 dice lingers after the initial 30 seconds until the target can properly wash their eyes. This weapon deals no Damage.

## Worn Armor

Armor reduces incoming Damage from most sources.

Armor from multiple sources cannot stack. Instead, the highest Armor Value is
used.

While bulkier Armors provide more protection, they also levy a dice penalty on
any physical actions attempted by their wearer. Note that dice penalties from
all sources stack.

### Layered/rugged Clothing
- **Armor Value:** 1
- **Penalty:** 0
- **Notes:** Examples include tough leather jackets and layered denim. Requires a Tiny Called Shot to circumvent.

### Reinforced Clothing
- **Armor Value:** 2, -1 against melee.
- **Penalty:** 0
- **Notes:** Technologically-advanced clothing designed as armor. Such as “bulletproof hoodies” or “bulletproof suits”. Requires a Small Called Shot to circumvent.

### Kevlar Vest
- **Armor Value:** 2
- **Penalty:** 0
- **Notes:** Ballistic armor that has been crafted & manufactured to fit under clothing, and be concealable (but not comfortable). Requires a Small Called Shot to circumvent.

### Plate Carrier
*10 lbs*
- **Armor Value:** 3
- **Penalty:** 1
- **Notes:** This armor is obvious and unusual, and it may cause social issues depending on the area. Requires a Small Called Shot to circumvent.

### Full Protection Vest / “FPV”
*20 lbs*
- **Armor Value:** 4. +1 against shrapnel / explosives.
- **Penalty:** 2
- **Notes:** This armor is obvious and unusual, and it may cause social issues depending on the area. Requires a Small Called Shot to circumvent.

### Full Armor System / FAS
*40 lbs*
- **Armor Value:** 5.
- **Penalty:** 3
- **Notes:** This armor is obvious and unusual, and it may cause social issues depending on the area. Requires a Tiny Called Shot to circumvent.

### Bomb Suit

### Full Plate

## Shields

Shields increase your Armor, stacking with the highest rating of any other Armor
you are wearing. However they incur a dice penalty to all physical Actions other
than Defending. All shields use up one hand, meaning you cannot use two-handed
weapons like rifles or greatswords with them.

Some shields are mobile (wheeled or mechanized), allowing them to move up to
10ft a Round while still providing Armor to their users, with no Penalty.

### Kite Shield, Small Riot Shield
- **Armor Bonus:** +1
- **Weight:** 10 lbs
- **Penalty:** 1

### Heater Shield, Riot Shield
- **Armor Bonus:** +2
- **Weight:** 15 lbs
- **Penalty:** 2

### Tower Shield, Ballistic Shield
- **Armor Bonus:** +3
- **Weight:** 35 lbs
- **Penalty:** 3 Some are mobile, allowing them to move up to 10ft a Round; functioning as “moving cover”

### Assault Shield
- **Armor Bonus:** +4
- **Weight:** 75 lbs
- **Penalty:** 4 Some are mobile, allowing them to move up to 10ft a Round; functioning as “moving cover”

Non-transparent shields may increase the Difficulty of Perception rolls.

Bucklers and armored wristbands do not provide extra Armor or take up a hand,
but they allow you to use Melee to Defend against Melee Attacks.
