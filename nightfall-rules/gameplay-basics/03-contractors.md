# Contractors

Players create and play characters known as Contractors. These are ambitious,
competent individuals who willingly risk their lives on
[Contracts](04-contracts.md) in exchange
for power. They face the illicit and unexplainable on a regular basis for an
opportunity to become something greater and change the world.

Each Contractor is unique. They may be a witch that lives in the woods, a social
media influencer with incredible persuasive skills, a stock broker who makes
deals with demons, or anything else. Their supernatural Powers and Equipment are
unique and tailor-made to fit their character concept.

Any group of Contractors is a grab bag of big personalities, diverse
backgrounds, and conflicting ambitions. They are united by a common goal, to
survive and claim victory on each Contract.

Contractors almost always start out as mundane humans with no supernatural
abilities. Certain character-creation options for advanced Players allow
Contractors to begin their first Contract with a Gift or a paranormal
background—or even as another species.

When someone becomes a Contractor, they are immediately Imbued. Imbuement alters
how they learn, makes them more resilient to mental trauma, and gives them an
uncanny ability to escape APCdeath (called Will to Survive).

Players fill out and maintain a
Character Sheet
for each of their Contractors. A Character Sheet defines the Contractor's
status, capabilities, and weaknesses. For example, Contractors have
Attributes that
reflect their core competencies and
Abilities that describe
their skills and knowledge. Players assign numerical values to Attributes and
Abilities during
character creation.

Players often maintain several Contractors, but they can never play more than
one at a time. No two Contractors owned by the same Player may ever meet or
communicate with each other.

## Contractor Status / Win Brackets

Contractors are divided into several Statuses based on the number of
[Contracts](04-contracts.md) they've won.
These statuses are used to determine a variety of things in Nightfall, but
mainly, they determine what brackets of Contracts a Contractor is able to play /
who they are able to interact with &/at when.

Contractors generally can only ever interact with Contractors within two
win-brackets of themselves.

For the purpose of determining a Contractor’s bracket; they count as the former
bracket. (A 3 win Contractor is considered 0-3, not 3-6.)

- #### Newbie: 0–3 Wins.

Newbie is where the Player & their Contractor’s career starts; it’s a test for
both the Player and the Player-Character to see if they have what it takes to
participate / continue their role in the Cosmic Conspiracy & also The Contract
in general.

Newbie games have somewhat linear structures to them; and generally you aren’t
expected to engage or handle obstacles that say, a police team (or similar)
couldn’t handle. Newbie games see you TP’d in, TP’d out on a safe completion;
they're simple enough.

A Newbie game might see you track a VIP from his home, to a gangland party on
the other side of town that rival oops (and police) are about to close in on;
with you expected to have him home at midnight, alive.

- Seasoned Gifts available from the get-go, along with a single free Gift
    Point.

  - Harbingers generally must transport Newbies to, and from the Contract (upon
    a safe completion).

  - Mandatory combat obstacles should generally be approachable by a team of
    Police Officers, or similar.

  - Contractors & their concepts are “malleable”; meaning (though it should
    still be recorded on-server / on-site) they may change / fluctuate somewhat
    freely.
    - A Police PC may become a FBI Agent, or similarly a wizard might become a
      hermetic sorcerer; as long as it's in the same “realm”.

  - Novice Rework unlocked at 4 Wins.

- #### Newbie-Novice: 3-6 Wins.

Newbie-Novice is like if you took Newbie, and complicated it further; this is
when more realistic things like “how am I getting here, and back” start to
matter & also when the obstacles & threats in a Contract amplify quite a bit.
Newbie Novice tends to not be so linear; with moving parts & obstacles which
disincentivize or shut down being purely a combat monster.

A Newbie-Novice game might see you track a VIP from his home, to a gangland
party surveilled by Federal Agents & about-to be converged on by criminal
opposition; all while you, yourself, are being followed / tracked by both Feds &
the criminal opposition.

- Newbie Novices generally must transport themselves to and from Contracts.

  - Mandatory combat obstacles should generally be approachable by a SWAT Team,
    or similar.

  - Novice Rework unlocked at 4 Wins.

- #### High Novice: 6-9 Wins.

High Novice is when headstrong holding W into a game’s obstacles stops being
possible & when it stops being “I should think outside the box” and more “I have
to think outside the box” to make it past a game’s obstacles.

A High Novice game might see you track a VIP down from a mutual contact of his
(who is a traitor, and informant), to a military-party in a foreign country
where spies, assassins, and rivals are waiting to converge on him; all while
you, yourself, are being followed / tracked by intelligence Agents (assassins) &
must find a way into / to stay at the party.

- Mandatory combat obstacles should generally be approachable by a Squad of
    Soldiers, or similar.

- #### Low Seasoned: 9-12 Wins.

Low Seasoned is generally when a game’s obstacles start necessitating that a
Contractor either has resources to draw apon from Downtime, or has learned from
their career to cheat the odds, and choose their challenges.

A Low Seasoned game might see you track a VIP down from just anywhere in the
world, all while intelligence agents and strike-squads are waiting to hit him,
all while intelligence agents zero in on attempts to investigate / papertrail to
him, while he's sitting in a fortified & secure bunker somewhere in a war-torn
(or similarly totalitarian) country… protected by the military, or similar, with
double agents inside preparing to assassinate him.

- Mandatory combat obstacles should generally be approachable by a special ops
  squad, or similar; the absolute best of the mundane world.
  - In other words; games no longer require that anyone except for specialized /
    highly skilled and resourceful, yet mostly mundane, people are able to solve
    / approach their obstacles at all.

  - Seasoned Rework unlocked at 10 Wins.

- #### Seasoned: 12-15 Wins.

Seasoned is where the bar raises & less are expected to do more; with only two
Contractors on each job, and with Contracts no longer requiring mundane
solutions / stipends: a Contractor’s entire career is tested…and some might just
choose instead to lose & go home, rather than risk it all.

A Seasoned game might see you track a VIP down from only an old picture to
somewhere in the world, all while superagents & feds wait to hit him / hit
anyone who comes after him, with the provided leads compromised & surveilled &
the VIP in question tucked away into some super facility in a foreign
totalitarian / war-torn nation: guarded not just by the military, but by
superagents and assets–some of which are preparing their own plays.

- Mandatory combat obstacles should generally be approachable by a super
    special ops squad, or similar.
    - In other words; games no longer require mundane solutions, or that mundane
      people are able to solve / approach their obstacles at all.

  - Seasoned Rework unlocked at 10 Wins.

- #### High Seasoned : 15-18 Wins.

High Seasoned is Seasoned but the bar has been raised a bit more. Losing a High
Seasoned game generally does not bode well for a Contract’s career; a bad High
Seasoned game can collapse or start the collapse of everything a Contractor’s
worked for.

A High Seasoned game might see you track down a VIP from an already-compromised
lead to somewhere in the world, a VIP who is an important and prominent person
for some major organization, with superagents & superassets at every turn
countering / serving as obstacles; with the VIP in question tucked away into a
super facility guarded by military, superagents–all of which will likely come
after a Contractor even after the Contract’s closing acts.

- Mandatory combat obstacles should generally be approachable by a specialized
  super special ops squad, or similar

- #### Vet Gate Game: 19 Wins.

The Vet Gate is a true test of both a Player & a Player Character. Vet Gate
Games are tailored specifically to both a Contractor, and the Creator; their
meant to be direct challenges of what makes a Contractor tick, tests of both of
the people behind a story & the decider on if that story will end, or if it will
come to serve as a pillar of the larger setting.

A Vet Gate Game should engage with the Contractor’s story in some meaningful and
major way; such as a Vatican Iscariot Inquisitor should have to go hunt down an
ancient esoteric cult of satanists who are birthing what could become the
antichrist.

Or a space-faring anti-alien PC should have to combat an expeditionary force of
extraterrestrials who have assimilated themselves into high society and places
of power within the world.

- Generally, should be specifically oriented to test the Contractor themself.

  - Winning this, and each game past it, unlocks one Veteran Gift Slot per win.

  - Vet-Gate Rework unlocked at 20 wins.

- #### Professional: 20-23 Wins.

Professional is largely a theoretical bracket in Nightfall; but generally
Professional games should challenge the status quo in some way / pit Contractors
against major elements of the setting / larger story of Nightfall.

These aren’t required to be specific to a Player Character, and can be mostly
general.

A Professional Contract might see a Contractor tossed against a great calamity
such as The Gun Devil (Chainsaw Man) or a Special Grade Curse embodying
literally Suicide, Plauge, Pestilence, Conquest, War–whatever.

- Mandatory combat obstacles should be generally approachable by your average
  country’s finest.

- #### Vet Test: 24 Wins.

While the main purpose of a Vet-Test (getting Veteran stuff) has already been
achieved; the milestone is significant enough that it warrants it’s own test.
Once again; a specific tailored game is required for the Contractor.

Perhaps a Vatican Iscariot Inquisitor might see themselves tossed against a cult
that has actually succeeded in bringing about an incarnation of the actual
Antichrist, or similarly an eldritch cult bringing about an incarnation of some
elder god.

Or maybe some Sorcerer has to square off the incarnation of an actual honest to
god deity; such as Amateratsu.

- Mandatory combat obstacles should be generally approachable by a superpower
  nation’s best.
  - Veteran Rework unlocked at 25 Wins.

- #### Veteran: 25 & + Wins.

Veteran is the point where a PC should have either fulfilled their Ambition, or
reached the point where continuing to Contract is merely sandboxing: a Veteran
PC is a cornerstone of the setting, a pillar of the setting, and deserves to be
immortalized within a setting.

Continuing to play past that is just to continue celebrating the PC and their
career; but you can pull from the criteria for Vet Test to gauge what’s needed.

- Mandatory combat obstacles should be generally approachable by the world’s
  finest.

Each Status tier unlocks new Gift advancement options, and some Statuses grant
other passive benefits.

Contractors can only play in Contracts with other Contractors within their “win
bracket” (listed above).

As a general rule; Contractors can only interact with Contractors within 2 win
brackets of themself. For example: a High Novice (6-9 Wins) could interact with
a Seasoned (12-15 Wins), but not a High Seasoned (15-18 Wins). That same High
Novice could interact with a Newbie (0-3 Wins), or a Newbie Novice (3-6 Wins).

## The Character Sheet

A Contractor's Character Sheet is the one-stop shop for information about them.
It tracks their stats, stock, and story.

The Character Sheet is filled out during
Contractor Creation
and maintained over the lifetime of the Contractor.

At gametime, Players use Character Sheets to reference their Contractors' stats
and track their well-being.

Character Sheets also provide background information on Contractors and track
their history, Experience, equipment, and Gifts.

## Online Character Sheets

Fully-featured online Character Sheets make creating and maintaining Contractors
as easy as possible.

The online Character Sheets do all the necessary math for the Player. They track
a Contractor's Contract and Gift history as well as all of their Experience
earned and spent. They contain helpful mouseover text and expandable rules
sections that are designed to be referenced at gametime.

Players may choose to mark their Character Sheets as private, which will hide
its content from everyone except their Playgroup leaders and the GM currently
running a Contract for them.

You can view an online Character Sheet for a near-Veteran Contractor
[here](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/characters/view/c/382/). You can fill out
your own Character Sheet by clicking
[here.](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/characters/create/)

### Physical Character Sheets

Online Character Sheets may be printed for in-person play using the "Print or
Download" button at the top of the page.

## Creating a Contractor

Creating a Contractor is simple. First, a Player comes up with a character
concept and fills out a Character Sheet. Next, their Playgroup Leader will
approve the sheet or request changes before the Contractor can be played.

Filling out a Character Sheet is an easy process using the online Character
Sheets. Simply visit the
[Create Contractor](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/contractor/create/) page and
follow the on-screen instructions.

There is also a
[printable, blank Character Sheet](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/contractor/blank/)
that can be filled out. However, if your goal is to use paper sheets, we still
recommend creating your Contractor with the
[online Character Sheet](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/contractor/create/) and
then printing out the result using the "Download or Print" button at the top of
the completed sheet.

Both the online and printed Character Sheets provide enough guidance that you
should not have to read further to create a Contractor. Read on for deeper
explanations of each element of the Character Sheet.

### Approving Contractors

Playgroup Leaders are responsible for approving Contractors created in their
Playgroup. For Contractors attending a Contract in another Playgroup, the GM may
allow or disallow their attendance at the start of a Contract at their
discretion.

GM Tip

Playgroup Leaders should not approve Contractors with disruptive or anti-fun
character concepts. Or those with stats that don't match their character concept
or background.

## Character Concept

Each Contractor should have a clearly defined character concept which informs
both how they behave as well as the sorts of
[Gifts](../gifts/01-overview.md) they will end up
using. A good concept has three pieces: Archetype, Paradigm, and Ambition.

### Archetype

Archetype is the Contractor's none-supernatural niche and style. Professions,
dominant personality traits, and subculture memberships make good Archetypes.

### Paradigm

A Contractor's Paradigm describes their latent supernatural potential. It guides
the sorts of Gifts they will receive and defines how their Powers work, although
Contractors can also receive Gifts related to their Archetype or Ambition.

### Ambition

A Contractor's Ambition is their driving goal. Why do they risk their life for
power? The best ambitions are lofty or impossible to achieve and put the
Contractor into conflict with the setting's status quo.

GM Tip

Character concepts are not set in stone. They often change dramatically during
their first few Contracts as they find their voice, and they continue developing
over the Contractor's career.

## Filling out a Character Sheet

After coming up with a Character concept, it's time to actually fill out the
Character Sheet.

### Filling in the Blanks

A series of blanks at the top of the sheet give critical at-a-glance information
about your Contractor. These include their name, age, ambition, and archetype.
Filling them in should be self-explanatory.

### Selecting Limits

Next, you must select three
Limits for your
Contractor. Limits define situations that test your Contractor's ability to
cope. Normal humans have the following three Limits by default: Anguish,
Atrocities, and Murder. Other options are provided to enable different Character
concepts, such as psychopaths, zealots, and faeries.

See the Limits section for
details.

### Attributes and Abilities

Attributes and Abilities define your Contractor's capabilities. The higher their
values, the more likely you are to succeed at the Actions you attempt.

Abilities are rated from 0–6, with the sixth level unpurchasable to Contractors
prior to obtaining Seasoned Status (10 wins)

0 - No Competence (Never done / not capable or trained at all) (Has zero
capability to use a gun &/or has never shot a gun ever/at all.) ("I've never
shot a gun before, I don't think I even could."

1 - Basic Capability, No Formal Exposure (Basic capability / competence for
shooting, a "knack") ("I could probably shoot a gun?")

2 - Capable, Informal Training (Taught to shoot by Parent, or has visited the
range at least a few times in their life) ("I've shot a gun before.")

3 - Competent, Irregular Training or Hobbyist (Hobby shooting, hunting, range
visits a few times a year, etc) ("I shoot every now & then")

4 - Employability, Baseline Formal Training (Police Academy, Shooter Drills,
Boot Camp, Job Shooting) ("My employment requires I be trained to shoot; it's a
core part of my job & I make a living with it.)

5 - Expertise, Advanced Formal Training (SWAT Training, Military Drills, War
Games, Regular Practice) ("My employment requires advanced training; it's a core
part of my job & my life; im an expert in the field).

6 - Mastery, World-Class Formal Training (Special Operations Training,
Government Programs, Mission Fielding) ("I've received some of the finest
training the world's to offer, regularly, rigorously; im the nation's finest.)

(6 is unobtainable Pre-Seasoned)

You must use
Experience (Exp) to
increase your starting Attributes and Abilities.

New Contractors start with 150
Experience.

The cost to increase an Attribute is its current rating × 5 (so buying the
second level costs 5 Exp, and buying the third costs 10 more Exp for a total of
15 Exp). Abilities cost 2 Exp for the first level, and then 2x their current
rating after that.

The costs to advance a stat when creating a Contractor remains the same after
the Contractor has started earning Experience from participating in Contracts.

All unused Experience is saved and can be spent during any
[Downtime](05-downtime.md).
