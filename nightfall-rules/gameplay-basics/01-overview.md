# Overview

This document contains a comprehensive list of rules needed to play in the
Nightfall Contract playgroup. New players interested in playing should at least
glance at this document, as it will provide a better understanding of how to
play.

## Gameplay Basics

The Contract is a tabletop roleplaying game about ambitious individuals who risk
their lives in deadly missions for supernatural gifts and a chance to change the
world. Those clever enough to survive are forged into some of the most fearsome
and influential beings in existence.

These "Contractors" come from many backgrounds and are driven by their own
individual ambitions. In order to succeed, they must rely on their wits,
resourcefulness, and even each other.

We all like to believe we’re resourceful and clever—that if we were in that
situation, we would have done the right thing.

Are you ready to learn whether or not that’s true?

## Gameplay Features

- Unique Characters
  - Contractors are highly individual. Using the custom Gift Builder, Players
    advance their Contractors with unique and flavorful Powers and Equipment.

- Solving Absenteeism
  - The Contract is great for inconsistent groups. Players can play and progress
    their Contractors regardless of who shows up to a given session.

- Rotating GMs
  - The role of the GameMaster (GM) rotates between sessions as desired,
    preventing "Forever GM" burnout.

- Shared Worlds
  - Each Playgroup can define its own custom setting or use the game's default
    setting, The Illumination. Online Character Sheets and community hubs built
    into the website allow dozens or even hundreds of Players to share a world.

## Game Structure

Whenever a group of people get together to play The Contract, it is called a
session. Most sessions require 3–5 people and last 3–6 hours. You can play with
whoever shows up for each session; it does not have to be the same group each
time.

Each session, one person assumes the role of the GameMaster (GM), and the rest
act as Players.

Each Player controls a personal character known as a Contractor. Contractors are
individuals who risk their lives in missions in exchange for supernatural
powers.

The GM acts as a referee, controlling the world and NPCs within it. They
organize and run the session, and narrate the experiences of the Contractors
based on their Players' choices.

The missions that Contractors attend are called Contracts. Each Contract starts
and ends in a single session. At the end of the Contract, the GM declares
whether each of the attending Contractors succeeded or failed.

When a Contractor claims victory on a Contract, they receive a Gift. Gifts are
custom-made abilities, items, or other powers which are the primary means of
advancing your contractor.

The time in-between contracts is called downtime. During this time, contractors
can pursue their ambitions, resolve loose ends potentially encountered during
contracts, or perform other actions they see fit. Contractors cannot earn Gifts
or Experience during their Downtimes.

## Basic Lore Overview

### Introduction

This version of the contract takes place in the “Nightfall Cinematic Universe”,
a setting where during the mid 20th century (around the end of World War II),
the existence of the supernatural at-large was discovered.

### World Overview

The Nightfall Cinematic Universe is a version of our modern-day earth where subtle supernatural phenomena have existed as long as life itself. In recent times, the existence of these supernatural occurrences has become more researched and widely known, however very little is understood about the forces behind them. The general population tends to live in fear of these supernatural forces, however there are groups who wish to exploit and utilize these powers.

### The Supernatural

As for the supernatural; supernatural phenomena are exceedingly rare. Generally, unless one actively seeks them, most people encounter the supernatural no more than once within their lifetime, with the chances of being directly affected being even lower. Most sources point to there being no more than 3,000,000 Superabled individuals globally–with the vast majority possessing merely minor supernatural capabilities or capacities.

## Playgroups

A Playgroup is a group of Players who regularly play The Contract together. A
Playgroup is also a group of
[Contractors](03-contractors.md) that
share a setting.

You can belong to any number of Playgroups, from small, private in-person groups
to sprawling online Playgroups with dozens or hundreds of members. However, each
Contractor must be "homed" in a single Playgroup.

Playgroup Leaders act as the head GM for their Playgroups, settling disputes,
approving [Gifts](../gifts/01-overview.md), and
generally preventing shenanigans. They are empowered to edit the Character
Sheets of the
[Contractors](03-contractors.md) in their
Playgroup, record or void
[Contracts](04-contracts.md), and define
their Playgroup's setting. Some Playgroups opt to use a tribunal of the three
most experienced GMs instead of a single Playgroup Leader.

The GameMaster's Manual includes a guide on
[Leading a Playgroup](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/gm-manual/#playgroup-leader).

The Contract's website provides an extensive host of tools that Playgroup
Leaders can use to build and manage their communities including configurable
roles and permissions, a newsfeed-style record of World Events that occur in the
setting of the group, membership management, and s to external communities.

Creating a new Playgroup is as easy as
[filling out the form](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/playgroup/create/). You
can also use the website to
[find online Playgroups looking for new Players](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/playgroup/find/).

Because Contractors are portable regardless of the setting, many Playgroup
Leaders permit their group's Contractors to
[participate in Contracts in other Playgroups](../01-situational-rules.md).

## The Website

What website? This one.

[https://www.thecontractrpg.com/](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/)

[TheContractRPG.com](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/) is far more than an online
guide for Players and GMs; it's The Contract's primary platform and a community
hub.

The website does three things:

1. Make The Contract as accessible and
   [easy to learn](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/info/learn-to-play/) as
   possible.

2. Allow Players to craft custom Powers and Equipment
   ([Gifts](../gifts/01-overview.md)) for their
   Contractors that are balanced, flavorful, and behave consistently no matter
   who is GMing.

3. Empower GMs and Playgroup leaders to manage large, inconsistent groups.

Features that make the game easy to learn and play include this searchable
Guidebook, the
[interactive How To Play Manual](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/info/how-to-play/),
[online Character Sheets](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/characters/create/),
the [Find a Playgroup](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/playgroup/find/) page, and
the
[Looking for Contracts](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/contract/looking-for-game/)
page. Players are notified when their Contractors have Experience and Gifts to
spend, and contextual rule references and hover text on the Character Sheet save
trips to the Guidebook.

Using this website, Players can easily form their own Playgroups. Playgroup
leaders are empowered to customize roles and permissions, establish their house
rules, and generate their own newsfeed-style record of World Events to build up
the setting. In addition, GMs save time with the fully automated record keeping
and can easily vet Contractors they've never seen before. Lastly, Players can
easily find Playgroups, GMs, and Contracts that match their personal
preferences.

The crown jewel of the website is the
[Gift Builder](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/powers/create/). No other tabletop
roleplaying game offers balanced, customizable powers and equipment that behave
consistently no matter who is GMing.

The website can be utilized as much or as little as you'd like. The Contract can
be played online or in person; the Scenarios, Character Sheets, Gifts, and
[rules reference](https://www.thecontractrpg.com/info/printable-quickstart/) can
be printed with the press of a button.
