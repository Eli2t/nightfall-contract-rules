# Actions

An Action is what a character does in a given ~3 second Round.

The most common Actions are:

- Attacking

- Using equipment

- Activating a Gift

- Sprinting, racing, climbing, performing other difficult movements

  Characters can choose to take any Action they'd like.

Actions that do not normally require a
[roll](../gameplay-basics/02-rolling-dice.md) will often
require one in combat due to the time pressure and higher stakes. You may be
able to climb a tree no problem normally, but can you do it while the police are
shooting at you?

## Long Actions

Some tasks can't reasonably be performed in 3 seconds and so take multiple
Actions. The GM informs the Player how many Actions their intended task might
take before they commit to doing it.

Because Combat tends to last only a few Rounds, it's not worth judging precisely
how many Actions it will take to perform any task that takes longer than 9
seconds or so. If a Contractor wants to pick a lock or make an omelet during
Combat, they'll be doing that until Combat ends and for some time after that.

## Attacking

A standard Attack represents an all-out attempt to kill your target.

To make an Attack, pick a target in range, specify how you'd like to hurt them,
then roll. The relevant Attribute, Ability, and Difficulty is determined by your
selected [weapon](../stocks/01-weapons-and-armor.md) or Gift.

GMs may apply Difficulty modifiers at their discretion, depending on the
circumstances. Shooting a stationary target is much easier than firing from a
moving vehicle during a rainstorm. See
[this list](05-hazards.md)
for common Difficulty modifiers.

Attacks are always treated as Contested Rolls. If your target does not React,
the Outcome of their defensive roll is treated as 0. If the Contested Outcome is
less than or equal to 0, that means the attack failed and no Damage is dealt.

If the Contested Outcome is positive, the
Damage you deal to the
target is equal to the Contested Outcome + Weapon Damage. All incoming Damage is
reduced by Armor, and the remainder is suffered as an
[Injury](../health/01-injuries.md) of the
appropriate Severity.

**GM Tip:**

Not every aggressive Action is an all-out attempt to kill. If a Contractor slaps
someone, tackles a target to the ground, or is fighting in a regulation boxing
match with gloves, don't calculate Damage as though they just caught the target
assaulting their daughter.

Note that attempts to knock targets out with violence DO count as attacks and
are incredibly risky. Hollywood-style knockouts require Gifts to perform safely.

### Called Shots

Inevitably someone will want to make a specific aimed attack targeting a hand,
an eye, or a weak spot. When such attacks go beyond flavoring and assume
strategic importance, the GM or Player declare a Called Shot. A called shot must
be declared before the dice are rolled.

The character then makes their attack as normal. When handling / resolving
Called Shots; use the following rules. The size of a target has nothing to do
with making Called Shots against a target, though may levy more or less
difficulty.

To hit a “small sized target” the size of a small arm or leg.

- A Contested Outcome of 3 is required in ranged combat.
- A Contested Outcome of 2 is required in melee combat.

To hit a “tiny sized target” the size of a hand, foot, head or heart:

- A Contested Outcome of 5 is required in ranged combat
- A Contested Outcome of 4 is required in melee combat

If the Called Shot is unsuccessful:

- The target is still hit, the damage is halved (rounded down) & the “Called
  Shot” is unsuccessful as instead the target is hit in a different location;
  typically center of mass.

If the Called Shot is successful:

- The target receives an appropriate Battle Scar in accordance with the location
  the Called Shot was made, along with other benefits levied at the GMs
  discretion; Armor penetration (or ignoring coverage-based material armor) &/or
  extra damage are common. Successful Called Shots against extremities or
  appendages limit the Damage to a Severity 5 Injury; excess Damage is ignored.

Example Called Shot vs a “small sized target”:

- A Successful Called Shot against someone’s arm or leg, if of enough damage to
  deal a Battlescar, deals a Battlescar 1 level higher than it usually would.
  (If someone rolled a 3 with a Called Shot with a Fire Axe (2 Weapon Damage) in
  melee combat; the target (without Armor) receives a Severity 5 Injury, but
  receives the Severity 6 Battlescar “Missing Leg” as their leg is dismembered
  (or otherwise disabled at the GM’s discretion)

Example Called Shot vs a “tiny sized target”:

- A Successful Called Shot against someone’s head or heart deals 2 additional
  damage, regardless of the “damage-capping” usual to Called Shots. Armor
  applies as usual to the location. (A Handgun would need 5 Contested Outcome &
  with 2 Weapon Damage; dealing 7 + 2 Damage, instantly killing most mundane
  men–unless they had Armor)

Some Gifts lower the amount needed for a Called Shot to 2/4 (Called it, Point
Control, etc); here’s how it changes things.

- (Small Sized Target) Melee = 0 Contested Outcome l Ranged = 1 Contested
  Outcome
- (Tiny Sized Target) Melee = 2 Contested Outcome l Ranged = 3 Contested Outcome

**GM Tip:**

Players often specify some detail about the manner in which they are attacking
("I take aim and fire" or "I slide under the creature's belly and stab
upwards"). When these descriptions are flavorful instead of strategic, interpret
them as standard all-out attacks without additional modifiers. Once the roll is
complete and Damage has been determined, narrate the attack based on the Outcome
(or allow the Players to narrate it).

This is a great opportunity to help Players feel badass. If doing the same
Action "in a boring way" is mechanically identical, let the Player do their
backflip-strike or what-have-you.

### Grappling and Choking

You may Grapple a target with Brawn + Melee, either as an Action or Clash. The
target may dodge, Defend, or Clash as normal. Whether or not they choose to
React, their Outcome to contest cannot be lower than their Brawn rating.

- If you specifically wish to choke a target (rendering them unable to speak)
  out, the Grapple roll is made at Difficulty 6 & requires a Called Shot against
  a Tiny-sized Target; 3 Contested Outcome in melee range.
  - If you wish to just Grapple the target & cover their mouth instead; roll @7.

- If the target does not contest or may otherwise take other Actions, the
  Grapple's Contested Outcome is subtracted from any physical Actions the target
  makes for the rest of that Round.
  - Outcome is not subtracted if the target Clashes with the Grappler.

  If your Contested Outcome is positive, the target becomes Grappled. Grappling
  deals no Damage.

- While your target is Grappled
  - Your target cannot use their Free Movement, Dash, or Sprint.

  - You may move as though you are lifting / hauling the target.

  - You may use your Action to proactively maintain the Grapple or attempt an
    unarmed or melee Attack against the target. For example, choking or stabbing
    them with your knife.
    - Choking is an unarmed Called Shot against the target’s Throat; it requires
      3 Contested Outcome (Melee Combat / Tiny-Sized Target) & ignores the
      majority of most mundane material Armors.
      - Choking a target “out” (to Incapacitation) deals a Stacking Severity
        Injury that starts at Contested Outcome (cannot start any higher than
        Severity 5) & increases by 1 for every Round maintained / any
        reapplications.
        - The Choker may choose to instead, “Choke to Kill”; if they do so,
          treat the Choke as a standard unarmed attack (-1 Damage) / Called Shot
          against the target’s throat
          - (Contested Outcome 3, if successful it ignores most material Armor &
            deals an additional 2 Damage)

      - Unsuccessful Called Shots to Choke a target follow the rules as usual;
        half damage, missing the target location of the throat & hitting,
        likely, center of mass instead.

  - You may use a Reaction to Defend or Clash against any physical Actions or
    Attacks made by your target. If you do, both of you roll Brawn + Melee
    instead of the Action’s standard roll.

  - Any attack the target makes against you is rolled at +1 Difficulty, +2
    Difficulty if against a target outside of melee range.

- Breaking Free
  - Grappled targets may use their Action or Clash to attempt to break free by
    rolling either Brawn or Dexterity + Melee. If their Outcome is positive,
    they are free of the Grapple. (If you contest, they may still choose to roll
    with Dexterity instead of Brawn).

  - If you are Grappling and you take an Injury Severity 3 or greater or if you
    receive a Battle Scar that would impact your ability to Grapple, the Grapple
    is broken.

  - If you attempt an Action unrelated to Grappling or melee combat with the
    target, the Grapple is broken.

### Garrotte

- When grappling with a garrotte such as piano wire, a silk scarf, or handcuffs,
  roll Brawn + Melee at Difficulty 6 & make a Called Shot against a Tiny-Sized
  Target; requiring 3 Contested Outcome.
  - If the Called Shot is successful, you Grapple the target using your Garotte
    & deal Damage equal to the contested Outcome (targeted on the neck)
    immediately, and as long as the target is grappled, you may spend an Action
    or Reaction to make another such Attack.

  - If the Called Shot is unsuccessful; you fail to Grapple the target, deal
    half damage & miss the target-location, likely hitting the center of mass.

- The target cannot attempt to escape as normal. They may escape by inflicting
  an appropriate Battle Scar, by incapacitating or killing you, or if you stop
  using your Actions to maintain the grapple.

- Otherwise: follows the normal rules for a Grapple.

### Disarms

Disarms are a special variant to Grapples that, instead of Grappling the target,
takes possession of/over something they are holding.

You may Disarm a target by rolling Brawn or Dexterity + Melee as either an
Action or Clash. The target may dodge, Defend, or Clash as normal. Whether or
not they choose to React, their Outcome to contest cannot be lower than their
Brawn rating.

- If the target does not contest, or otherwise chooses to take other Actions,
  the Disarm’s Contested Outcome is subtracted from any physical Actions the
  target makes for the rest of that Round.

- If your Contested Outcome is positive, you may take possession of their
  Weapon, or something on their immediate person, and/or launch said possession
  Contested Outcome \* 5 feet away.
  - If you dedicate your Quick Actions prior (as is required to take Quick
    Actions in conjunction with Actions); you may use your Action to Disarm
    someone & then also, instead of launching their disarmed possession away,
    choose to stow, or equip their possession.
    - IE: Disarming someone’s magical amulet by declaring the Quick Action
      prior, rolling with -2 Dice, succeeding & disarming them of their Amulet /
      putting it on yourself (or in your pockets) all in one round.

- Any active attempt to maintain a Grapple may also be used to in tandem,
  perform a Disarm.

### Shoves, Trips & Pins

You may Shove or Trip a target by rolling Brawn or Dexterity + Melee as either
an Action or Clash. The target may dodge, Defend, or Clash as normal. Whether or
not they choose to React, their Outcome to contest cannot be lower than their
Brawn rating.

- If the target does not contest, or otherwise chooses to take other Actions,
  the Shove or Trip’s Contested Outcome is subtracted from any physical Actions
  the target makes for the rest of that Round.

- Any active attempt to maintain a Grapple may also be used to in tandem perform
  a Shove or Trip.

If you successfully shove your target; you may knock them Contested Outcome \*
2.5 feet.

Alternatively, you may choose to shove / trip your target prone; knocking them
down. A target that is knocked down must spend a Quick Action to use their
Movement to get back up; if the target is grappled, they cannot stand back up so
long as you actively maintain the grapple.

A target that is grappled & also knocked down, is considered “Pinned”.

- A “Pinned” target suffers +1 Difficulty to attack their Grappler, +2
  Difficulty to attack anyone not in melee range & +1 Difficulty to all physical
  Actions due to being pinned. A Pinned Target essentially suffers +2 Difficulty
  to attack their Grappler.

- A Pinned target’s most effective ways to escape their Grappler are the
  following.
  - Breaking free from the Grapple itself (they’d only roll @ +1 Difficulty to
    break free, for being knocked down).

  - Dealing a Severity 3 to their Grappler (+2 Difficulty to attack their
    Grappler).

  - Having an ally to help them break out of the Grapple, attack their Grappler,
    or otherwise occupy their Grappler so they can escape.
    - It only takes a single Contested Outcome to break free from the Grapple
      itself, with a roll @ +1 Difficulty; meaning if the Grappler is occupied,
      it is all the more easy to escape.

### Throws

If picking up / "Lifting" a target would require Concentration; you must have
already Grappled your target to throw them--otherwise, you may roll the lower of
your Brawn + Melee or Athletics to grab & throw a target.

The target may dodge or Defend / Clash as usual, but regardless of whether they
choose to do so, cannot roll an Outcome lower than their Brawn Rating.

- (Brawn _ 5 = Encumbrance) _ 3 = Weight you must Concentrate to Lift) \* 5 =
  Light Maximum)

- The target may be thrown Contested Outcome \* 5ft & has a Weapon Damage equal
  to half their Brawn (rounded down). Upon hitting a target or surface; the
  thrown target takes the Contested Outcome + Weapon Damage in full.
  - The thrown target may use a Reaction to “soften” their landing, or otherwise
    dodge / Defend or Clash with their thrower, to resist being thrown.

  - Any target at risk of being "hit" by the thrown target may make their own
    Dodge, Defend or Clash.
    - If this target Clashes; they may, at the GM’s discretion, throw the target
      back, make an attack & stop the thrown target, make an attack through the
      thrown target, or make an attack & dodge the thrown target.
      - If the thrown target is attacked in this; they may make an available
        dodge, Defend or Clash of their own.

- Any active attempt to maintain a Grapple may also be used to perform a Throw.

**GM Tip:**

In Nightfall(& also real contact “fighting” sports) it’s well accepted that once
a Grappler has someone in a hold; it’s incredibly difficult to escape without
substantial skill differences, strategy, or speedy thinking. Most escape a
grapple by attacking their Grappler well before they get “pinned down”, turning
the grapple or by otherwise managing to use whatever weapon they still have to
escape the Grapple.

Skilled Grapplers can easily turn close encounters with assailants armed with
military gear, raging Sanguinphagic Vampires, out-of-control Zoanthropic
Werepeople, or even physically incarnate Spirits or Demons, into cinematic
close-combats where they grab, pin, chokeslam & throw said assailants against
the wall.

Remember that characters have a “safety floor” equal to their Brawn against
Grapples, Garottes, Disarms, Shoves & Trips & so on and so forth. While Grapples
(& Grapplers) are powerful; note that it often is much more efficient &
effective to just make an all-out attack against someone with a melee weapon (or
enhanced unarmed attacks) to kill them, then it is to go through the whole
process of Grappling, Pinning, Choking & Throwing.

## Quick Actions and Multitasking

### Quick Actions

Simple tasks that are unlikely to fail and don't warrant a full Action can be
performed as Quick Actions. This includes drawing a holstered weapon, standing
up, buckling your seatbelt, and closing a door.

Quick Actions do not need to be
[rolled](../gameplay-basics/02-rolling-dice.md), and each
Quick Action subtracts 2 dice from that Round's main Action or Reaction (to a
minimum of 0).

If a Contractor wishes to take their main Action and then perform a Quick
Action, their main Action's roll is still at -2 dice. If they didn't take a dice
penalty ahead of time, they'll have to wait until next Round to perform their
Quick Action.

Characters may take a maximum of 3 Quick Actions per Round, and taking 3 Quick
Actions in a Round uses up your Action for that Round. "Free" Quick Actions from
Gifts do not count towards this limit.

### Mental Actions

Can you think and act at the same time? It turns out, the answer is "not
really."

Whether or not mental Actions require a roll is up to the GM's discretion.
Generally any concerted thinking ("What do I know about this creature?", "How
can I solve this door puzzle before the scarabs eat us?") is treated like a
regular Action.

In contrast, general awareness or "passive" mental Actions (Do they notice the
shark swimming up behind them?) can be rolled with the character's full dice
pool without subtracting dice from the rest of their Action.

### Holding Actions

Characters may "hold" their Action by specifying an Action and a condition under
which the Action will occur. For example, they may say "I want to shoot the
first person who turns this corner." or "I will slit the hostage's throat if
they struggle." or "I will follow Jennifer and stab anyone who tries to attack
us."

You must specify the Action and condition to the GM when holding an Action.

A held Action lasts until the character's next Action. However, actually
following through on a held Action will use up the current Round's Action
(regardless of when the Action was originally held). For example, Jeff declares
"I will pull the lever on the electric chair if the convict starts to turn into
a werewolf." The current Round ends and the next Round begins. The convict
(whose Initiative puts their turn before Jeff's) then begins to sprout fur and
fangs. Jeff will pull the lever, and doing so will forfeit his Action for the
current Round.

Held Actions resolve before the Action that triggered them unless the triggering
Action cannot normally be reacted to (e.g. a surprise attack) or the triggering
action is itself an attack, in which case it turns into a Clash.

Characters may make a
[Reaction](03-reactions.md), even if they
are currently holding an Action. Doing so forfeits the held Action.

### Aiming Rules

Characters may reduce the Difficulty of an attack by spending an Action aiming
at a stationary or predictably-moving target–this requires Concentration on your
target (While concentrating you can only take Free Actions, a single Quick
Action per Round & can only move up to 10ft a round; Disrupting events (like
taking Damage, being sufficiently distracted, or having their focus disrupted)
cause Concentration to end.)

When Concentrating in this way (dropping Concentration is a free action), any
and all Perception Rolls you make to notice something other than your target, or
not within the immediate view of your target, (Such as an attacker from
elsewhere, or someone sneaking up on you, or your peripherals) are made at +2
Difficulty.

When Aiming at a target, characters are effectively suspending a readied Attack
against that target. For every round past the one spent initially Aiming, they
may roll at - X Difficulty.

Characters that Aim must specify a specific trigger to watch out for, but if the
target “triggers” or otherwise chooses to Attack the Aiming character & the
Aiming character decides to use their readied Attack; it becomes a Clash.
Characters that are Aiming may change their trigger on their initiative.

### Weapon Handling Rules

All Weapons w/ Weapon Damage utilize Handling Rules, which are the following: In
order to use a Weapon w/out increased Difficulty your Brawn Rating must match
its Weapon Damage–if not, you suffer a Difficulty Increase equal to the
difference.

If a Weapon had 3 Weapon Damage, and a character had 2 Brawn: they'd roll at +1
Difficulty. If they instead had 1 Brawn: they'd roll at +2 Difficulty.

For Weapons that have increased Weapon Damages due to Gifts, or supernatural
Effects–you use the higher of either the base Weapon Damage, or half of the
total Weapon Damage rounded up. If it is an Extraordinary Weapon, and has the
Buster Drawback, you instead utilize the full Weapon Damage for Handling.

### Targets in Melee Combat / Mobs

Firearms require a Called Shot to hit targets who are actively engaged in melee
combat with others. Failure deals half damage to one target at the GM's
discretion. If the target is engaged in melee combat with only a singular other
target; roll High / Low, if you fail, you hit an unintended target (Such as (if
one of them were) your teammate.)

For a target engaged in melee combat against a singular person, a Called Shot
against a Small Target is required. Requires 3 Contested Outcome for Ranged
Combat.

For a target engaged in melee combat against many targets (such as a Mob), a
Called Shot against a Tiny Target is required. Requires 5 Contested Outcome for
Ranged Combat.

### Full-Auto Sweeps

Fully automatic weapons, such as machine guns and some rifles, are able to make
a special type of attack.

Attackers wielding fully automatic weapons may "sweep" an area; covering 5
square feet per 5 rounds expended. Most standard automatic weapons cover up to
about an area of 30 x 30 feet w/ a full magazine. The attacker rolls at +2
Difficulty and must expend the rest of their magazine. This attack will damage
anyone within its area of effect if they do not dodge or evade.

### Clashing with Firearms

Most weapons lack the range that Firearms have and thus are unable to Defend or
Clash against Firearms at-range normally–Firearms may Clash against other
Firearm attacks, but may not Defend without Gifts or Effects.

If you have your gun drawn, you may Clash with other Firearms attacks at-range.
(Note: some Effects will let you instead Defend, against even things that are
not Firearms)

Resolution for different scenarios:

- Clash Winners: The winner(s) of the Clash gets to shoot; Losers forfeit their
  Action (or Reactions).

- Multiple Clashes: If multiple Clashes occur; resolve them in the order of the
  attacking character's initiative.

"What'd you say?" Firing unsuppressed firearms in enclosed locations can cause
temporary hearing loss, and it is entirely fair to levy temporary Perception
penalties on affected characters where hearing is a factor.

**GM Tip:**

Baptize that pistol. Firearms that have been fired underwater or submerged for a
while generally need to be disassembled and cleaned before they can be fired
reliably again. Bullets cannot travel effectively through water at any range,
although you may allow for damage if the barrel is pressed against a target.
